================================================================================
                  ARTICLE 4 - INTEREST
================================================================================

4.1 COUPON RATE

    The NCDs shall carry interest at the Coupon Rate specified in the
    Term Sheet, payable annually on the Interest Payment Date.

4.2 INTEREST PAYMENT DATES

    Series A: November 30 of each year (2022, 2023, 2024)
    Series B: June 15 of each year (2023, 2024, 2025)

4.3 INTEREST CALCULATION

    Interest shall be calculated on the outstanding principal amount at the
    Coupon Rate on the basis of a year of 365 days and the actual number of
    days elapsed.

4.4 DAY COUNT CONVENTION

    Actual/Actual

4.5 DEFAULT INTEREST

    In case of delay in payment of interest or principal, the Company shall
    pay additional interest at the rate of 2% per annum above the Coupon Rate
    for the period of default.

4.6 TAX DEDUCTION

    The Company shall deduct tax at source on interest payments as per
    applicable laws. The Company shall issue TDS certificates to NCD holders.

================================================================================
                   ARTICLE 5 - REDEMPTION
================================================================================

5.1 REDEMPTION AT MATURITY

    The NCDs shall be redeemed at par on the Maturity Date. The redemption
    amount along with final interest payment shall be paid to the registered
    holders through their bank accounts linked to their demat accounts.

5.2 EARLY REDEMPTION

    (a) CALL OPTION: The Company may, at its sole discretion, redeem the
        NCDs before the Maturity Date by giving at least 21 days prior notice
        to the NCD holders. Early redemption shall be at a premium of 0.50%
        over the face value.

    (b) PUT OPTION: NCD holders may, by giving at least 21 days prior notice
        to the Company, require the Company to redeem their NCDs before the
        Maturity Date in case of:
        - Downgrade of credit rating below investment grade
        - Change in control of the Company
        - Material breach of covenants not cured within 30 days

5.3 RECORD DATE

    The Company shall fix a record date at least 15 days before any interest
    payment date or redemption date. Only persons whose names appear in the
    register of debenture holders on the record date shall be entitled to
    receive payment.

================================================================================
                    ARTICLE 6 - SECURITY
================================================================================

6.1 NATURE OF SECURITY

    The NCDs are secured by:

    FIRST PARI PASSU CHARGE on the Company's present and future Loan
    Receivables with minimum security cover of 1.10 times the outstanding
    NCD amount.

6.2 SECURITY DOCUMENTS

    The Company shall execute:
    (a) Deed of Hypothecation in favor of the Trustee
    (b) Power of Attorney in favor of the Trustee

6.3 ASSET COVER

    The Company shall maintain asset cover of at least 1.10 times at all
    times. The Company shall submit monthly certificates to the Trustee
    confirming the asset cover.

    If the asset cover falls below 1.10x for 2 consecutive months, the
    Company shall:
    (a) Provide additional security within 30 days, or
    (b) Redeem part of the NCDs to restore the asset cover

6.4 REGISTRATION OF CHARGE

    The charge shall be registered with:
    (a) Registrar of Companies, Maharashtra within 30 days
    (b) Central Registry of Securitisation (CERSAI) within 30 days

6.5 SHARING OF SECURITY

    The Trustee shall share the security on pari passu basis with other
    secured lenders to the Company as per the inter-creditor agreement.

================================================================================
                 ARTICLE 7 - CREDIT RATING
================================================================================

7.1 INITIAL RATING

    The NCDs have been rated by CRISIL Ratings as follows:
    - Rating: CRISIL A- (pronounced "CRISIL A minus")
    - Outlook: Stable

7.2 RATING RATIONALE

    The rating reflects the Company's:
    - Adequate capital position with CRAR of 17.8%
    - Diversified funding profile
    - Experienced management team

    The rating is constrained by:
    - Higher than peer NPA levels
    - Geographic concentration in Maharashtra

7.3 ANNUAL SURVEILLANCE

    The rating shall be reviewed annually by CRISIL. The Company shall:
    (a) Cooperate with the rating agency
    (b) Provide all information required
    (c) Inform the Trustee immediately of any rating action

7.4 RATING DOWNGRADE

    In case the rating is downgraded below investment grade (below BBB-):
    (a) The Trustee shall convene a meeting of NCD holders
    (b) NCD holders may exercise the put option

================================================================================

8.1 LISTING

    Listing Details:

8.2 TRADING

    in minimum lots of Rs. 1,00,00,000.

8.3 LISTING COMPLIANCE

    The Company shall comply with all listing requirements including:
    (a) Payment of annual listing fees
    (b) Submission of half-yearly financial results
    (c) Disclosure of material events
    (d) Maintenance of minimum 25% public holding (not applicable for
        privately placed NCDs)

================================================================================
            ARTICLE 9 - ROLE OF DEBENTURE TRUSTEE
================================================================================

9.1 APPOINTMENT

    The Company hereby appoints Vistra ITCL (India) Limited as the Debenture
    Trustee for the NCD holders. The Trustee accepts the appointment.

9.2 DUTIES OF TRUSTEE

    The Trustee shall:

    (a) Protect the interests of the NCD holders
    (b) Ensure creation of security and maintenance of asset cover
    (c) Monitor compliance by the Company with covenants
    (d) Call meetings of NCD holders when required
    (e) Enforce the security in case of default
    (f) Take actions on behalf of NCD holders

9.3 RIGHTS OF TRUSTEE

    The Trustee shall have the right to:

    (a) Inspect the books and records of the Company
    (b) Appoint a nominee on the Board of the Company in case of default
    (c) Enforce the security upon occurrence of Event of Default
    (d) Seek information from the Company

9.4 TRUSTEE FEE

    The Company shall pay to the Trustee an annual fee as agreed between
    the parties, plus reimbursement of out-of-pocket expenses.

================================================================================
               ARTICLE 10 - COMPANY COVENANTS
================================================================================

10.1 AFFIRMATIVE COVENANTS

     The Company shall:

     (a) FINANCIAL COVENANTS:
         - Maintain CRAR of at least 15%
         - Maintain Debt to Equity ratio below 4:1
         - Maintain asset cover of 1.10x
         - Maintain Net NPA below 6%

     (b) REPORTING TO TRUSTEE:
         - Monthly asset cover certificate
         - Quarterly financial statements within 45 days
         - Annual audited financial statements within 90 days
         - Intimation of any material event within 2 days

     (c) OTHER:
         - Maintain RBI registration
         - Comply with all applicable laws
         - Maintain adequate insurance
         - Pay interest and principal on due dates

10.2 NEGATIVE COVENANTS

     Without the Trustee's prior written consent, the Company shall not:

     (a) Create any security ranking prior to the NCD security
     (b) Change its constitution or management (except in ordinary course)
     (c) Undertake any merger, amalgamation, or demerger
     (d) Declare dividend if financial covenants are breached
     (e) Dispose of substantial assets

================================================================================
               ARTICLE 11 - EVENTS OF DEFAULT
================================================================================

11.1 EVENTS OF DEFAULT

     Each of the following is an Event of Default:

     (a) Failure to pay interest or principal within 7 days of due date
     (b) Breach of any covenant not cured within 30 days of notice
     (c) Breach of any financial covenant for 2 consecutive quarters
     (d) Cross-default (default under any other borrowing exceeding Rs. 5 Cr)
     (e) Credit rating downgrade below investment grade
     (f) Cancellation or suspension of RBI registration
     (g) Insolvency, winding up, or similar proceedings
     (h) Asset cover falling below 1x for 2 consecutive months
     (i) Any representation or warranty being materially incorrect

11.2 CONSEQUENCES OF DEFAULT

     Upon occurrence of an Event of Default, the Trustee may:

     (a) Declare all outstanding NCDs immediately due and payable
     (b) Enforce the security
     (c) Appoint a nominee director on the Board
     (d) Take any other action to protect NCD holders' interests

================================================================================
              ARTICLE 12 - MEETINGS OF NCD HOLDERS
================================================================================

12.1 CONVENING MEETINGS

     Meetings of NCD holders may be convened by:
     (a) The Trustee
     (b) The Company
     (c) NCD holders holding at least 10% of outstanding NCDs

12.2 QUORUM

     The quorum shall be NCD holders holding at least 25% of outstanding NCDs.

12.3 VOTING

     Each NCD holder shall have one vote for each NCD held.
     Ordinary Resolution: Simple majority
     Special Resolution: 75% majority

13.2 JURISDICTION

13.3 NOTICES

     All notices shall be sent to the addresses specified in this Trust Deed.
