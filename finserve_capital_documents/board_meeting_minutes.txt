ITEM NO. 3: REVIEW OF FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE - Q2 FY 2024-25
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CFO presented the unaudited financial results for Q2 FY 2024-25 (July-
September 2024):

Key Highlights:
- Total AUM: Rs. 428 Crores (Growth: 9.2% YoY)
- Net Interest Income: Rs. 8.2 Crores
- Profit After Tax: Rs. 4.8 Crores
- Net Interest Margin: 7.2%
- Cost to Income Ratio: 42%
- Gross NPA: 9.3%
- Net NPA: 5.1%
- CRAR: 18.2%

Discussion: Ms. Meera Krishnan raised concerns about the NPA levels being
higher than peer average of 6-7%. The management explained the initiatives
being taken to improve collections and the expected improvement in coming
quarters.

RESOLVED THAT the unaudited financial results for Q2 FY 2024-25 be and are
hereby approved and taken on record.

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the same be published and filed with stock exchanges
where NCDs of the Company are listed.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM NO. 4: REVIEW OF RBI COMPLIANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Chief Compliance Officer presented the quarterly RBI compliance report:

1. Capital Adequacy:
   - CRAR: 18.2% (Requirement: 15%) - Compliant
   - Tier I: 13.8% (Requirement: 10%) - Compliant
   - NOF: Rs. 142 Crores (Requirement: Rs. 2 Cr) - Compliant

2. Prudential Norms:
   - Single Borrower Limit: 22.5% (Limit: 25%) - Compliant
   - Asset Classification done as per IRACP norms - Compliant
   - Provisioning: Rs. 27 Crores maintained

3. Returns Filed:
   - NBS-7 for Q1 FY 2024-25: Filed on July 15, 2024
   - ALM Returns: Filed monthly
   - All returns filed within timelines

4. Pending RBI Observations:
   - 2 observations from June 2023 inspection under monitoring
   - Expected closure by December 2024

RESOLVED THAT the RBI Compliance Report for Q2 FY 2024-25 be and is hereby
noted and approved.

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the Chief Compliance Officer be authorized to continue
monitoring compliance and report any deviations immediately to the Board.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM NO. 5: REVIEW OF RISK MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Chief Risk Officer presented the quarterly risk report:

1. Credit Risk:
   - Portfolio at Risk (PAR 90+): 9.3%
   - Concentration Risk: Top 20 exposures = 28% of portfolio
   - Geographic concentration: Maharashtra 38% (monitoring required)

2. Liquidity Risk:
   - LCR: 152% (comfortable)
   - No ALM mismatches in critical buckets
   - Unutilized bank lines: Rs. 45 Crores

3. Operational Risk:
   - 3 operational risk incidents in Q2 (all resolved)
   - No material fraud detected
   - Collections system enhancement 85% complete

4. Cyber Security:
   - 2 minor incidents detected and resolved
   - No data breach
   - Penetration testing completed - all vulnerabilities addressed

RESOLVED THAT the Risk Management Report for Q2 FY 2024-25 be and is hereby
noted and approved.

Mr. Suresh Iyer recommended increasing monitoring of geographic concentration.
Management agreed to present a diversification plan at the next meeting.

ACTION: Management to present geographic diversification plan at next meeting.

ITEM NO. 7: REVIEW OF NPA MANAGEMENT AND COLLECTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Head of Collections presented the NPA position and recovery initiatives:

1. Current NPA Position:
   - Gross NPA: Rs. 40 Crores (9.3%)
   - Net NPA: Rs. 22 Crores (5.1%)
   - Provisioning Coverage: 68%

2. Movement During Q2:
   - Opening Gross NPA: Rs. 38 Crores
   - Fresh Slippages: Rs. 8 Crores
   - Recoveries/Upgrades: Rs. 4 Crores
   - Write-offs: Rs. 2 Crores
   - Closing Gross NPA: Rs. 40 Crores

3. Recovery Initiatives:
   - Enhanced field collection team by 15 members
   - Implemented AI-based early warning system
   - 47 legal cases filed, expected recovery Rs. 9 Crores
   - OTS approved for 12 accounts worth Rs. 3 Crores

4. Target: Reduce Gross NPA to 8% by March 2025

RESOLVED THAT the NPA Management Report be noted and the initiatives taken
by management be approved.

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT the OTS proposals approved by the Loan Recovery
Committee be ratified.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM NO. 8: ANNUAL REVIEW OF CREDIT RATING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The representative from CRISIL presented the annual credit rating review:

Current Ratings:
- Long-term: CRISIL A- / Stable
- Short-term: CRISIL A2+ (for Commercial Paper)

Key Strengths:
- Adequate capital position with CRAR of 18.2%
- Comfortable liquidity with LCR of 152%
- Diversified funding profile
- Experienced management team

Key Concerns:
- Higher than peer NPA levels
- Geographic concentration in Maharashtra
- Competitive pressure on margins

Rating Outlook: Stable

The rating will be reviewed again in September 2025.

RESOLVED THAT the CRISIL Rating Review be noted and management be directed
to address the concerns raised by the rating agency.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ITEM NO. 9: APPROVAL OF BORROWING PROPOSAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The CFO presented a proposal for additional borrowing to support growth:

Proposal: Term Loan of Rs. 50 Crores from State Bank of India
Tenure: 3 years
Interest Rate: MCLR + 150 bps (currently 9.50%)
Security: First pari passu charge on loan receivables
Purpose: To fund AUM growth in FY 2024-25

Post-borrowing, total borrowings will be Rs. 345 Crores, within sanctioned
limits and maintaining Debt/Equity ratio below 3x.

RESOLVED THAT approval be and is hereby accorded for availing term loan
facility of Rs. 50 Crores from State Bank of India on terms as presented.

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT Mr. Rajesh Kumar Mehta, Managing Director, and
Mr. Vikram Joshi, CFO, be and are hereby authorized to negotiate and
finalize the terms, execute loan agreements and related documents, create
security, and do all acts necessary for availing the said facility.
