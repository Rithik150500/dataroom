================================================================================
                      POLICY OVERVIEW
================================================================================

1.1 OBJECTIVE

    This Credit Policy establishes the framework for lending operations of
    FinServe Capital Limited to ensure:
    - Prudent credit risk management
    - Consistent underwriting standards
    - Compliance with RBI regulations
    - Optimal portfolio quality
    - Fair and transparent lending practices

1.2 SCOPE

    This policy applies to all lending products offered by the Company:
    - SME Term Loans
    - Vehicle Finance
    - MSME Working Capital
    - Loan Against Property (planned)

1.3 GUIDING PRINCIPLES

    - Risk-adjusted pricing
    - Portfolio diversification
    - Adequate security cover
    - Proper due diligence
    - Compliance with Fair Practices Code

================================================================================
               III. CREDIT ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

3.1 CREDIT APPRAISAL PROCESS

    Step 1: Application Screening
    - Document collection
    - Preliminary eligibility check
    - Credit bureau check (CIBIL/Experian/CRIF/Equifax)

    Step 2: Field Investigation
    - Business visit
    - Residence verification
    - Reference check
    - Stock/asset verification

    Step 3: Financial Analysis
    - Ratio analysis
    - Cash flow assessment
    - Debt service capability
    - Collateral valuation

    Step 4: Credit Scoring
    - Internal credit scorecard
    - Risk rating assignment
    - Pricing determination

    Step 5: Sanctioning
    - As per delegated powers
    - Terms and conditions

3.2 CREDIT SCORING MODEL

    Parameter                          Max Score
    --------------------------------------------
    Business Vintage                      15
    Financial Performance                 25
    Banking/Credit History                20
    Management/Promoter Profile           15
    Industry/Sector Risk                  10
    Collateral Coverage                   15
    TOTAL                                100

    Score Range          Rating      Decision
    -----------------------------------------
    80 - 100            A           Approve
    65 - 79             B           Approve with conditions
    50 - 64             C           Conditional/Enhanced security
    Below 50            D           Decline

3.3 CREDIT BUREAU CRITERIA

    Minimum CIBIL Score for Individuals: 650
    Minimum CMR Rank for Companies: CMR 6 or better

    Negative criteria:
    - DPD > 90 days in last 12 months
    - Written-off accounts
    - Settled accounts without NOC
    - Active wilful defaulter tag

5.3 GEOGRAPHIC CONCENTRATION

    State                   Maximum Exposure
    ----------------------------------------
    Single State            40%

    Current Position:
    - Maharashtra: 38% (monitoring required)
    - Karnataka: 22%
    - Gujarat: 18%
    - Others: 22%

    Management Action: Expansion to Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Rajasthan
    to reduce Maharashtra concentration.

5.4 PROHIBITED EXPOSURES

    The Company shall NOT lend to:
    - Directors or their relatives (RBI restriction)
    - Companies where directors are interested
    - Sensitive sectors (speculation, wilful defaulters)
    - Bridge loans to companies for acquisition
    - Financing for shares/debentures purchase

================================================================================
              VI. COLLATERAL AND SECURITY
================================================================================

6.1 COLLATERAL REQUIREMENTS

    Loan Type               Collateral Requirement
    ----------------------------------------------
    > Rs. 25 Lakhs         Mandatory collateral
    Rs. 10 - 25 Lakhs      Collateral or CGTMSE
    < Rs. 10 Lakhs         CGTMSE coverage preferred

6.2 ACCEPTABLE COLLATERAL

    Primary:
    - Commercial property (LTV: 60-70%)
    - Residential property (LTV: 65-75%)
    - Plant & Machinery (LTV: 50-60%)
    - Vehicles (LTV: 70-85%)

    Additional:
    - Fixed Deposits (Margin: 10-15%)
    - Government Securities (Margin: 10%)
    - LIC Policies (Surrender value)

6.3 VALUATION REQUIREMENTS

    - Valuation by empanelled valuers
    - Distress value and market value both
    - Re-valuation every 3 years
    - Immediate valuation if value deterioration suspected

6.4 SECURITY DOCUMENTATION

    - Registered mortgage for immovable property
    - Hypothecation agreement for movables
    - CERSAI registration within 30 days
    - Personal guarantee of promoters/directors
    - Corporate guarantee where applicable

================================================================================
               VII. INTEREST RATE AND PRICING
================================================================================

7.1 PRICING MODEL

    Interest Rate = Base Rate + Credit Risk Premium + Operating Cost Margin

    Base Rate: Linked to cost of funds
    Current Base Rate: 10.5% p.a.

7.2 RISK-BASED PRICING

    Credit Rating          Risk Premium
    ------------------------------------
    A                      2.0% - 3.0%
    B                      3.0% - 5.0%
    C                      5.0% - 7.5%

7.3 PRICING APPROVAL

    - Standard pricing as per grid: Branch level
    - Pricing concession up to 50 bps: Regional Manager
    - Pricing concession > 50 bps: Credit Committee

================================================================================
             VIII. LOAN MONITORING AND REVIEW
================================================================================

8.1 POST-DISBURSEMENT MONITORING

    Frequency           Activity
    -------------------------------------------
    Monthly             EMI/repayment tracking
    Quarterly           Stock/debtors statement
    Half-yearly         Financial statements
    Annually            Valuation update (if required)
                        Insurance renewal check
                        Credit bureau refresh

8.2 EARLY WARNING SIGNALS

    Financial:
    - Declining sales/profits
    - Deteriorating ratios
    - Delay in submission of financials
    - Bouncing of cheques

    Operational:
    - Key management changes
    - Loss of major customer/supplier
    - Regulatory/legal issues
    - Labor disputes

    Account Conduct:
    - Irregular repayments
    - Frequent ECS returns
    - Request for restructuring
    - Downgrade by rating agency

8.3 SPECIAL MENTION ACCOUNTS (SMA)

    Category        Days Past Due        Action
    ------------------------------------------------
    SMA-0           1 - 30 days          Reminder calls
    SMA-1           31 - 60 days         Collection visits
                                         Send to legal review
    SMA-2           61 - 90 days         Recall notice
                                         Initiate recovery

================================================================================
              IX. NPA MANAGEMENT AND PROVISIONING
================================================================================

9.1 ASSET CLASSIFICATION (AS PER RBI IRACP NORMS)

    Category              Definition              Provisioning
    ----------------------------------------------------------
    Standard              < 90 days overdue       0.40%
    Sub-standard          > 90 days overdue       15%
    Doubtful - 1 yr       Sub-std for 1 yr        25%
    Doubtful - 2 yr       Sub-std for 2 yrs       40%
    Doubtful - 3 yr       Sub-std for 3 yrs       100%
    Loss                  Identified as loss      100%

9.2 CURRENT NPA POSITION

    As of September 30, 2025:
    - Gross NPA: Rs. 40 Crores (9.3%)
    - Net NPA: Rs. 22 Crores (5.1%)
    - Provisioning Coverage: 68%

    Target: Reduce Gross NPA to 8% by March 2025

9.3 RECOVERY AND RESOLUTION

    Methods:
    - Soft collections
    - Legal action (DRT, Civil Court)
    - SARFAESI action (for secured loans > Rs. 1 Lakh)
    - One-Time Settlement (OTS)
    - Assignment/sale to ARCs

    OTS Authority:
    - Up to 20% write-off: Chief Credit Officer
    - 20-40% write-off: Credit Committee
    - > 40% write-off: Board
