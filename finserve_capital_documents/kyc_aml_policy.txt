                         PREAMBLE
================================================================================

This KYC/AML Policy has been framed in accordance with:

1. Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 (PMLA)
2. Prevention of Money Laundering (Maintenance of Records) Rules, 2005
3. RBI Master Direction - Know Your Customer (KYC) Direction, 2016
4. Master Direction - Non-Banking Financial Company - Scale Based Regulation
   Directions, 2023

The policy aims to:
- Prevent money laundering and terrorist financing
- Ensure proper customer identification and due diligence
- Maintain records as required by law
- Report suspicious transactions to FIU-IND

================================================================================
               I. CUSTOMER ACCEPTANCE POLICY
================================================================================

1.1 RISK CATEGORIZATION

    The Company shall categorize customers into three risk categories:

    Category        Description                    Due Diligence
    ---------------------------------------------------------------
    LOW RISK        - Salaried individuals         Simplified
                    - Government employees
                    - Small business < Rs. 50 lakhs
                    - Loans < Rs. 10 lakhs

    MEDIUM RISK     - Self-employed individuals    Standard
                    - SMEs with turnover
                      Rs. 50 lakhs - 10 Cr
                    - Loans Rs. 10 - 50 lakhs

    HIGH RISK       - Non-residents                Enhanced
                    - Companies with complex
                      structures
                    - Trusts/NGOs
                    - Cash-intensive businesses
                    - Politically Exposed Persons
                    - Loans > Rs. 50 lakhs

1.2 PROHIBITED CUSTOMERS

    The Company shall NOT establish relationship with:

    (a) Anonymous or fictitious accounts
    (b) Persons/entities on UN/OFAC sanctions lists
    (c) Persons on RBI's list of wilful defaulters
    (d) Shell companies without genuine business
    (e) Persons convicted of financial crimes
    (f) Declared terrorists or terrorist organizations
    (g) Persons against whom regulatory/law enforcement action is ongoing

1.3 CUSTOMER IDENTIFICATION

    Every customer shall be identified through valid documents and
    verification before establishing business relationship.

================================================================================
            II. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
================================================================================

2.1 INDIVIDUAL CUSTOMERS

    Identity Documents (any one):
    - Aadhaar Card
    - Passport
    - Voter's ID
    - Driving License
    - PAN Card
    - NREGA Job Card

    Address Documents (any one):
    - Aadhaar Card
    - Utility Bills (not older than 3 months)
    - Bank Statement (not older than 3 months)
    - Property Tax Receipt
    - Registered Rent Agreement

    Additional Requirements:
    - Recent photograph
    - Signature verification
    - Contact number verification (OTP)
    - Email verification

2.2 NON-INDIVIDUAL CUSTOMERS

    A. COMPANIES

    - Certificate of Incorporation
    - Memorandum and Articles of Association
    - PAN Card
    - Board Resolution for borrowing
    - List of Directors with KYC
    - Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBO) with >10% holding
    - Latest audited financials
    - GST Registration

    B. PARTNERSHIP FIRMS

    - Partnership Deed
    - PAN of firm
    - KYC of all partners
    - Letter of authority

    C. LLPs

    - Certificate of Incorporation
    - LLP Agreement
    - PAN
    - KYC of all Designated Partners

    D. TRUSTS/NGOs

    - Trust Deed/Registration Certificate
    - PAN
    - KYC of all trustees
    - List of beneficiaries (if determinable)

2.3 ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)

    For high-risk customers, additional measures include:

    (a) Senior management approval for relationship
    (b) Source of funds verification
    (c) Purpose of transaction
    (d) More frequent KYC updates (annual)
    (e) Enhanced transaction monitoring
    (f) Site visits

2.4 POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEPs)

    Definition: Individuals entrusted with prominent public functions by a
    foreign country, including Heads of State, senior politicians, senior
    government officials, judicial or military officials, senior executives
    of state-owned corporations.

    EDD Measures for PEPs:
    - Senior management approval
    - Source of wealth and funds
    - Enhanced ongoing monitoring
    - Annual review
    - Additional background checks

2.5 ULTIMATE BENEFICIAL OWNER (UBO)

    The Company shall identify UBOs as:
    - Natural person(s) who ultimately own/control > 10% of shares/capital
    - Natural person who exercises control through other means
    - If no natural person identified, the senior managing official

================================================================================
                III. KYC VERIFICATION
================================================================================

3.1 VERIFICATION METHODS

    (a) OVD (Officially Valid Documents) verification
    (b) C-KYC (Central KYC Registry) search
    (c) CERSAI search
    (d) Video KYC (V-KYC) for digital onboarding
    (e) In-person verification for high-risk

3.2 C-KYC COMPLIANCE

    The Company shall:
    (a) Search C-KYC registry before customer onboarding
    (b) Download KYC if available, after customer consent
    (c) Upload KYC records to C-KYC within 10 days of account opening
    (d) Update C-KYC for any changes in customer information

3.3 VIDEO KYC (V-KYC)

    For digital onboarding, V-KYC shall be conducted as per RBI guidelines:

    (a) Seamless and real-time audio-video interaction
    (b) Customer to display OVD and PAN during video
    (c) Live photograph capture
    (d) Geo-tagging
    (e) Auditor official to verify documents in real-time
    (f) Recording of entire V-KYC process
    (g) Concurrent audit of V-KYC accounts

================================================================================
             IV. ONGOING CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE
================================================================================

4.1 KYC UPDATE FREQUENCY

    Risk Category           Update Frequency
    ----------------------------------------
    High Risk               Every 2 years
    Medium Risk             Every 8 years
    Low Risk                Every 10 years

4.2 TRANSACTION MONITORING

    The Company shall monitor customer transactions on an ongoing basis to:
    - Ensure consistency with customer profile
    - Detect unusual or suspicious patterns
    - Update risk categorization

4.3 TRIGGER-BASED REVIEW

    KYC review shall be triggered by:
    - Material change in customer profile
    - Large value transactions inconsistent with profile
    - Regulatory/law enforcement inquiry
    - Adverse media reports
    - Sanction list updates

================================================================================
             V. RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

5.1 RECORDS TO BE MAINTAINED

    (a) All KYC documents and identification records
    (b) Transaction records including:
        - Date of transaction
        - Nature and value of transaction
        - Parties involved
    (c) Correspondence relating to transactions
    (d) Results of any analysis undertaken

5.2 RETENTION PERIOD

    Records shall be maintained for:
    - During currency of account/relationship
    - 5 years after account closure/transaction completion

5.3 RETRIEVAL OF RECORDS

    Records shall be made available to competent authorities within
    specified timelines (generally 3 days).

================================================================================
           VI. SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION REPORTING
================================================================================

6.1 DEFINITION OF SUSPICIOUS TRANSACTION

    A transaction where there is reasonable ground to suspect that:
    - It involves proceeds of an offence
    - It is attempted to evade legal requirements
    - It is designed to avoid reporting threshold
    - It is connected to terrorist financing

6.2 RED FLAGS

    The following shall be treated as red flags:

    IDENTITY-RELATED:
    - Reluctance to provide information
    - Inconsistent documents
    - Multiple addresses
    - Frequent change in KYC details

    TRANSACTION-RELATED:
    - Cash transactions near reporting threshold
    - Rapid movement of funds without business rationale
    - Transactions with sanctioned countries
    - Repayment from unknown sources
    - Request for early closure without logical reason

    BUSINESS-RELATED:
    - Business inconsistent with stated profile
    - Unusual cash-intensive operations
    - Sudden increase in business activity
    - Complex structures without clear purpose

6.3 REPORTING TO FIU-IND

    The Company shall report to Financial Intelligence Unit - India (FIU-IND):

    Report Type              Threshold/Condition           Timeline
    ----------------------------------------------------------------
    Cash Transaction         All cash transactions        Within 15
    Report (CTR)             > Rs. 10 lakhs per month     days of
                                                          month end

    Suspicious               Any suspicious transaction   Within 7 days
    Transaction              regardless of amount         of detection
    Report (STR)

    Non-Profit               Transactions with NPOs       Within 15
    Organization Report      > Rs. 10 lakhs               days

    Cross Border Wire        Wire transfers > Rs. 5       Within 15
    Transfer Report          lakhs (individual) or        days of
                             Rs. 25 lakhs (others)        month end

6.4 CONFIDENTIALITY OF STR

    - STR filing shall be kept strictly confidential
    - No employee shall disclose STR to customer (tipping-off prohibited)
    - STR to be filed even if no transaction done (attempted transaction)
