OBSERVATION 1: ASSET CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
In 5 accounts totaling Rs. 2.3 Crores, NPA classification was delayed
beyond 90 days. The accounts were classified as NPA at 95-102 days instead
of the mandatory 90 days.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. The delay was due to manual reconciliation
processes which have since been automated.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Implemented automated NPA flagging system effective July 1, 2023
2. System now automatically classifies accounts as NPA on 91st day
3. Daily exception report generated for accounts approaching 90 days
4. Manual override requires Credit Committee approval with documented reasons

Evidence Submitted:
- System screenshots showing automated classification
- Policy note on NPA classification
- Sample reports generated

Impact:
- 5 accounts reclassified to correct NPA date
- Additional provision of Rs. 12 Lakhs made

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 2: PROVISIONING ADEQUACY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Provisioning for 3 doubtful accounts (outstanding Rs. 85 Lakhs) was at 15%
instead of the required 25% for assets classified as doubtful for less
than one year.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the shortfall in provisioning. The error occurred due to
incorrect configuration in the provisioning module.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Rectified provisioning percentages in the system
2. Made additional provision of Rs. 8.5 Lakhs
3. Implemented quarterly review of provisioning adequacy by Audit Committee
4. Internal audit to verify provisioning in each audit cycle

Evidence Submitted:
- Board resolution for additional provision
- System configuration screenshots
- Audit Committee minutes

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 3: INCOME RECOGNITION ON NPAs
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Interest income of Rs. 4.2 Lakhs was recognized on 2 NPA accounts after
the date of NPA classification.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. Interest was inadvertently recognized due
to system configuration error.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Interest income reversed in Q1 FY 2023-24
2. System configured to automatically stop interest recognition on NPA date
3. Reconciliation performed for all NPA accounts for FY 2022-23

Evidence Submitted:
- Journal entries for reversal
- System configuration
- Reconciliation statement

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 4: KYC UPDATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
KYC updation for 48 customers (4.2% of portfolio) was overdue. Of these,
15 were high-risk customers where KYC should be updated every 2 years.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. The delay was due to challenges in
contacting customers and obtaining updated documents.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Dedicated team formed for KYC updation
2. Multiple contact attempts made (calls, SMS, email, physical visit)
3. 42 out of 48 customers' KYC updated as of September 15, 2023
4. Remaining 6 customers' accounts placed under "no further credit" status
5. Automated KYC renewal alerts implemented - 60, 30, 15 days before due date

Evidence Submitted:
- Updated KYC records
- Customer contact logs
- System alert configuration

Current Status:
- All 48 customers' KYC now updated
- Ongoing monitoring to prevent recurrence

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 5: STR FILING DELAY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
1 Suspicious Transaction Report was filed with delay of 12 days (filed on
19th day instead of within 7 days of detection).

Company's Response:

We regret the delay in STR filing. The delay occurred due to extended
internal investigation to gather sufficient information.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. STR workflow revised to ensure filing within 7 days
2. Preliminary STR to be filed within 7 days even if investigation ongoing
3. Principal Officer to escalate immediately upon detection
4. Daily tracking of suspicious transactions implemented

Evidence Submitted:
- Revised STR filing workflow
- Training records
- Register of suspicious transactions

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 6: FAIR PRACTICES CODE - SANCTION LETTER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
In 8 loan files examined, sanction letters did not contain annualized
effective rate of interest as required under Fair Practices Code.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. While interest rate was mentioned, the
annualized effective rate (considering compounding and fees) was not
separately disclosed.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Sanction letter format revised to include:
   - Nominal interest rate
   - Annualized effective rate (APR)
   - Processing fees and charges
   - EMI schedule
2. All future sanction letters in revised format from July 1, 2023
3. Training provided to credit team

Evidence Submitted:
- Revised sanction letter format
- Sample sanction letters in new format
- Training records

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 7: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
3 customer complaints were resolved beyond the Company's stated timeline
of 15 days (resolved in 18, 21, and 25 days respectively).

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the delay. The complaints required detailed investigation
and coordination with third parties (collection agents).

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Additional resources deployed in grievance cell
2. Escalation matrix implemented with daily tracking
3. If resolution requires > 15 days, interim response to be provided
4. Root cause analysis for delayed complaints

Evidence Submitted:
- Complaint resolution reports
- Escalation matrix
- Revised grievance policy

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 8: IT SYSTEMS - DATA BACKUP
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Backup testing was not conducted for 2 consecutive quarters. The last
backup restoration test was conducted in October 2022.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. Backup testing was missed due to system
migration project which was ongoing during Q3 and Q4 FY 2022-23.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Backup restoration test conducted in July 2023 - successful
2. Quarterly backup testing now mandated in IT Policy
3. Calendar alerts set for quarterly testing
4. IT Strategy Committee to review backup status quarterly

Evidence Submitted:
- Backup restoration test reports (July 2023, October 2023)
- IT Policy extract
- IT Strategy Committee minutes

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

OBSERVATION 9: CYBER SECURITY - VULNERABILITY ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT) was last conducted
in September 2022. As per RBI IT Framework, VAPT should be conducted annually.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. Annual VAPT was due in September 2023 and
has been scheduled.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. VAPT conducted in August 2023 by CERT-In empanelled auditor
2. 12 vulnerabilities identified (3 high, 5 medium, 4 low)
3. All high-severity vulnerabilities addressed within 30 days
4. Medium vulnerabilities addressed within 60 days
5. Annual VAPT calendar established

Evidence Submitted:
- VAPT Report August 2023
- Vulnerability closure certificate
- Annual IT calendar

Current Status:
- 3 high vulnerabilities: Closed
- 5 medium vulnerabilities: 3 closed, 2 in progress (due by December 2023)
- 4 low vulnerabilities: Under remediation

Status: UNDER MONITORING
        (High vulnerabilities closed, medium ones being tracked)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 10: CORPORATE GOVERNANCE - AUDIT COMMITTEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Audit Committee meeting in Q3 FY 2022-23 was attended by only 2 members
(quorum is 3). The minutes do not record reason for non-attendance.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. One member was unable to attend due to
medical emergency but the reason was not recorded in minutes.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Minutes format revised to mandatorily record reason for non-attendance
2. Alternate members to be identified for committees
3. Video conferencing to be facilitated for participation
4. Company Secretary to follow up on attendance

Evidence Submitted:
- Revised minutes format
- Subsequent Audit Committee minutes with improved attendance

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 11: CORPORATE GOVERNANCE - RISK COMMITTEE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Risk Management Committee did not discuss stress testing results in 2 out
of 4 meetings in FY 2022-23.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the observation. Stress testing was conducted but results
were presented to the Board directly instead of Risk Committee.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Risk Committee charter amended to mandatorily include stress testing
2. Stress testing results now presented to Risk Committee first
3. Agenda for Risk Committee standardized
4. Board Note format for Risk Committee revised

Evidence Submitted:
- Revised Risk Committee charter
- Subsequent Risk Committee minutes with stress testing discussion

Status: CLOSED (RBI letter dated November 15, 2023)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OBSERVATION 12: LARGE EXPOSURE REPORTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RBI Observation:
Large Exposure Report submitted to RBI contained errors in 2 entries.
The exposure amount for one group was understated by Rs. 45 Lakhs due to
non-inclusion of off-balance sheet exposure.

Company's Response:

We acknowledge the error. The off-balance sheet exposure (bank guarantee
backed by the Company) was inadvertently omitted.

Corrective Action Taken:
1. Revised Large Exposure Report submitted to RBI on August 15, 2023
2. Reporting template enhanced to capture off-balance sheet items
3. Maker-checker process implemented for regulatory returns
4. Quarterly reconciliation with internal records

Evidence Submitted:
- Revised Large Exposure Report
- Enhanced reporting template
- Reconciliation report

Current Status:
- Revised report submitted
- RBI reviewing the revised report

Status: UNDER MONITORING
        (Awaiting RBI confirmation on revised report)
