                        PRELIMINARY

1. TABLE F NOT TO APPLY

    The regulations contained in Table F of Schedule I to the Companies Act,
    2013 shall not apply to this Company except in so far as the same are
    repeated or contained in these Articles.

        or as altered from time to time;

    (c) "Auditors" means the Statutory Auditors for the time being of the
        Company;

    (d) "Board" or "Board of Directors" means the Board of Directors of the
        Company;

    (e) "Company" means FinServe Capital Limited;

    (f) "Directors" means the Directors for the time being of the Company;

    (g) "Dividend" includes interim dividend;

    (h) "Member" means a member of the Company;

    (i) "Meeting" includes a meeting convened through video conferencing or
        other audio visual means;

    (j) "Office" means the Registered Office for the time being of the Company;

    (k) "Paid up" includes credited as paid up;

    (l) "Person" includes body corporate;

    (m) "RBI" means the Reserve Bank of India;

    (n) "NBFC Regulations" means the Master Directions, guidelines, circulars

    (q) "Secretary" means the Company Secretary of the Company;

    (r) "Year" means the financial year of the Company;

    (s) Words importing the singular number shall include the plural number

================================================================================
                     PART I - SHARE CAPITAL
================================================================================

3. AUTHORIZED SHARE CAPITAL

    The Authorized Share Capital of the Company is Rs. 75,00,00,000 (Rupees
    Seventy Five Crores Only) divided into 7,50,00,000 Equity Shares of
    Rs. 10 each with power to increase, reduce, consolidate, subdivide or
    otherwise reorganize the capital.

4. ISSUE OF SHARES

    (a) Subject to the provisions of the Act and these Articles, the shares
        shall be under the control of the Board who may allot or otherwise
        dispose of the same to such persons, on such terms and conditions
        and at such times as the Board thinks fit.

    (b) The Company may issue shares with differential voting rights as to
        dividend, voting or otherwise, subject to such conditions as may be
        prescribed by the Rules.

    (c) RBI COMPLIANCE: Any allotment of shares resulting in any person/
        entity holding 26% or more of the paid-up capital shall require
        prior approval of the Reserve Bank of India as per NBFC Regulations.

5. RESTRICTION ON TRANSFER OF SHARES

    (a) The instrument of transfer of any share shall be in writing and all
        the provisions of the Act and the Rules made thereunder shall be duly
        complied with.

    (b) RBI APPROVAL: Transfer of shares resulting in acquisition of 26% or
        more of the paid-up capital by any person/entity shall require prior
        approval of RBI.

    (c) PROMOTER SHAREHOLDING: The Company shall ensure that an identifiable
        promoter group holds at least 26% of the paid-up capital at all times
        as required by RBI for NBFCs.

6. CALLS ON SHARES

    (a) The Board may from time to time make calls upon the Members in respect
        of any money unpaid on their shares.

    (b) Each Member shall, on receiving at least 14 days notice specifying
        the time or times and place of payment, pay to the Company at the
        time or times and place so specified the amount called on his shares.

7. LIEN ON SHARES

    The Company shall have a first and paramount lien on every share for all
    moneys called or payable at a fixed time in respect of such share.

8. FORFEITURE OF SHARES

    If a Member fails to pay any call or installment of a call on the day
    appointed for payment thereof, the Board may, at any time thereafter
    during such time as any part of the call or installment remains unpaid,
    serve a notice on him requiring payment.

================================================================================
                PART II - BOARD OF DIRECTORS
================================================================================

9. NUMBER OF DIRECTORS

    (a) The Company shall have not less than 3 and not more than 15 Directors.

    (b) RBI REQUIREMENT: The Company being a Middle Layer NBFC shall have at
        least 3 Independent Directors on the Board.

    (c) At least one Woman Director shall be on the Board as required under
        Section 149(1) of the Act.

10. COMPOSITION OF BOARD

    As per RBI requirements for Middle Layer NBFCs and the Companies Act:

    (a) Not less than half of the Directors shall be Non-Executive Directors;

    (b) At least one-third of the total Directors shall be Independent Directors;

    (c) At least one Director shall be a Woman Director;

    (d) The composition shall meet "fit and proper" criteria as prescribed by RBI.

11. FIT AND PROPER CRITERIA

    (a) Every Director shall meet the "fit and proper" criteria prescribed by
        RBI for NBFCs, which includes:

        (i)   Integrity, reputation and character;
        (ii)  Absence of convictions and adverse orders;
        (iii) Sound financial integrity;
        (iv)  No disqualification under the Companies Act.

    (b) The Board shall obtain a declaration and undertaking from each Director
        confirming compliance with "fit and proper" criteria before appointment.

    (c) Any Director who ceases to meet the "fit and proper" criteria shall
        forthwith cease to hold office.

12. APPOINTMENT OF DIRECTORS

    (a) Directors shall be appointed in accordance with the Act and NBFC
        Regulations.

    (b) The appointment of Managing Director, Whole-Time Director or Manager
        shall require compliance with Section 196 and Schedule V of the Act.

    (c) RBI APPROVAL: Appointment of Managing Director/CEO or any change in
        the management of the Company shall require prior information/approval
        of RBI as per extant guidelines.

13. DISQUALIFICATION OF DIRECTORS

    In addition to the disqualifications under Section 164 of the Act, a person
    shall be disqualified for appointment as Director if:

    (a) The person does not meet RBI's "fit and proper" criteria;

    (b) The person has been removed or debarred by RBI or any other financial
        sector regulator;

    (c) The Company of which such person was a Director has been prohibited
        by RBI from carrying on NBFC business.

14. REMOVAL OF DIRECTORS

    (a) The Company may by ordinary resolution remove any Director before the
        expiry of his term, subject to compliance with Section 169 of the Act.

    (b) If RBI directs removal of any Director on "fit and proper" or other
        grounds, the Board shall take immediate steps to comply with such
        direction.

15. BOARD MEETINGS

    (a) The Board shall meet at least once in every quarter and at least 4
        meetings shall be held in every year.

    (b) The gap between two Board meetings shall not exceed 120 days.

    (c) Quorum: One-third of total strength or 2 Directors, whichever is higher.

    (d) Meetings may be held through video conferencing or other audio visual
        means as permitted under the Act and Rules.

16. POWERS OF THE BOARD

    Subject to the provisions of the Act and these Articles, the Board shall
    have powers to:

    (a) Make calls on Members in respect of money unpaid on shares;

    (b) Issue debentures and other securities;

    (c) Borrow moneys otherwise than on debentures;

    (d) Invest the funds of the Company;

    (e) Make loans and advances in the ordinary course of NBFC business;

    (f) Approve credit policies, underwriting standards, and risk management
        policies subject to RBI guidelines;

    (g) Appoint and fix remuneration of Key Managerial Personnel subject to
        applicable provisions;

    (h) Delegate powers to Committees of the Board.

================================================================================
              PART III - BOARD COMMITTEES (RBI MANDATED)
================================================================================

17. AUDIT COMMITTEE

    (a) The Company shall constitute an Audit Committee as required under
        Section 177 of the Act and RBI guidelines for NBFCs.

    (b) Composition: Minimum 3 Directors with majority being Independent
        Directors. Chairman shall be an Independent Director.

    (c) Functions: As prescribed in Section 177 of the Act and RBI guidelines.

18. NOMINATION AND REMUNERATION COMMITTEE

    (a) The Company shall constitute a Nomination and Remuneration Committee
        as required under Section 178 of the Act.

    (b) Composition: 3 or more Non-Executive Directors with at least half
        being Independent Directors.

    (c) Functions: As prescribed in Section 178 of the Act and RBI guidelines.

19. RISK MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE

    (a) RBI MANDATED: The Company being a Middle Layer NBFC shall constitute
        a Risk Management Committee.

    (b) Composition: As prescribed by RBI - to include Independent Directors,
        Chief Risk Officer and other senior management.

    (c) Functions:
        (i)   Oversee credit risk, market risk, liquidity risk and operational
              risk;
        (ii)  Review risk appetite and tolerance;
        (iii) Monitor adherence to risk limits;
        (iv)  Review stress testing and scenario analysis;
        (v)   Report to Board on risk exposures.

20. ASSET LIABILITY MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE (ALCO)

    (a) RBI MANDATED: The Company shall constitute an ALCO.

    (b) Composition: To include CEO/MD, CFO, Head of Treasury, Chief Risk
        Officer and other senior management.

    (c) Functions:
        (i)   Monitor asset-liability mismatches;
        (ii)  Ensure adequate liquidity;
        (iii) Manage interest rate risk;
        (iv)  Review ALM position and gaps.

21. IT STRATEGY COMMITTEE

    (a) The Company shall constitute an IT Strategy Committee as per RBI's
        IT Framework for NBFCs.

    (b) Functions: Approve IT strategy, review cyber security, oversee
        technology investments.

================================================================================
            PART IV - KEY MANAGERIAL PERSONNEL
================================================================================

22. APPOINTMENT OF KEY MANAGERIAL PERSONNEL

    The Company shall have the following Key Managerial Personnel:

    (a) Managing Director or Chief Executive Officer;
    (b) Company Secretary;
    (c) Chief Financial Officer;
    (d) Whole-Time Directors (if any).

    RBI REQUIREMENT: Additionally, the Company shall appoint:
    (e) Chief Risk Officer (CRO);
    (f) Chief Compliance Officer (CCO);
    (g) Principal Officer for PMLA compliance.

23. RESPONSIBILITIES OF KEY MANAGERIAL PERSONNEL

    (a) CHIEF RISK OFFICER: Shall report to the Board/Risk Management Committee
        and shall be responsible for implementing risk management framework.

    (b) CHIEF COMPLIANCE OFFICER: Shall be responsible for compliance with all
        applicable laws and RBI regulations.

    (c) PRINCIPAL OFFICER: Shall be responsible for furnishing information to
        FIU-IND under PMLA.

================================================================================
                  PART V - RBI COMPLIANCE
================================================================================

24. GENERAL COMPLIANCE OBLIGATION

    The Company shall comply with all applicable provisions of:

    (a) Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934;
    (b) Master Directions issued by RBI for NBFCs;
    (c) All other circulars, guidelines and notifications issued by RBI.

25. CAPITAL ADEQUACY

    (a) The Company shall maintain CRAR of at least 15% or such other
        percentage as prescribed by RBI.

    (b) Tier I capital shall be at least 10% of risk-weighted assets.

    (c) Capital adequacy shall be calculated and monitored quarterly.

26. NET OWNED FUNDS

    The Company shall maintain Net Owned Funds (NOF) of at least Rs. 2 Crores
    or such other amount as prescribed by RBI from time to time.

27. CONCENTRATION NORMS

    The Company shall comply with exposure norms:

    (a) Single borrower exposure: Not exceeding 25% of Owned Funds;
    (b) Group borrower exposure: Not exceeding 40% of Owned Funds.

28. ASSET CLASSIFICATION AND PROVISIONING

    The Company shall classify assets and make provisions as per IRACP norms
    prescribed by RBI.

29. FAIR PRACTICES CODE

    The Company shall adopt and implement a Fair Practices Code approved by
    the Board as prescribed by RBI.

30. KYC/AML COMPLIANCE

    The Company shall comply with Know Your Customer and Anti-Money Laundering
    guidelines issued by RBI and PMLA requirements.

31. RETURNS AND REPORTING

    The Company shall submit all returns, reports and information to RBI
    within prescribed timelines including:

    (a) Quarterly returns on prudential norms;
    (b) ALM returns;
    (c) Annual statutory auditor's certificate;
    (d) Any other returns as prescribed.

================================================================================
                PART VI - GENERAL MEETINGS
================================================================================

32. ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING

    The Company shall hold Annual General Meeting as required under Section
    96 of the Act.

33. NOTICE OF GENERAL MEETING

    A general meeting may be called by giving not less than 21 clear days
    notice in writing.

34. QUORUM FOR GENERAL MEETING

    (a) For Public Company: 5 Members personally present if number of members
        is not more than 1000; 15 if more than 1000 but not more than 5000;
        30 if number exceeds 5000.

35. VOTING RIGHTS

    Every Member shall have voting rights in proportion to his share in the
    paid-up equity share capital of the Company.

================================================================================
                  PART VII - DIVIDENDS
================================================================================

36. DIVIDEND

    (a) The Company may declare dividends subject to the provisions of Section
        123 of the Act.

    (b) RBI RESTRICTION: The Company shall declare dividends in accordance
        with RBI guidelines for NBFCs which may restrict dividend payment
        based on NPA levels and CRAR.

37. TRANSFER TO RESERVES

    Before declaring dividend, the Company shall transfer to reserves at least
    20% of the profits as required by RBI for NBFCs.

================================================================================
                    PART VIII - ACCOUNTS
================================================================================

38. BOOKS OF ACCOUNT

    The Company shall keep proper books of account as required under Section
    128 of the Act.

39. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS

    The Company shall prepare financial statements in accordance with Indian
    Accounting Standards (Ind AS) applicable to NBFCs.

40. AUDIT

    (a) The accounts shall be audited by Statutory Auditors appointed under
        Section 139 of the Act.

    (b) Additional audits as required by RBI:
        (i)   Internal Audit;
        (ii)  Concurrent Audit;
        (iii) Information Systems Audit;
        (iv)  Secretarial Audit.

================================================================================
                PART IX - WINDING UP
================================================================================

41. WINDING UP

    (a) The Company may be wound up voluntarily or by the Tribunal as per the
        provisions of the Act.

    (b) RBI REQUIREMENT: No scheme of arrangement, amalgamation, merger,
        reconstruction, demerger or winding up shall be sanctioned without
        prior approval of RBI.

================================================================================
                     PART X - INDEMNITY
================================================================================

42. INDEMNITY

    Every Director, Manager, Secretary and other Officer of the Company shall
    be indemnified by the Company against all losses and expenses incurred by
    him in defending any proceedings in which judgment is given in his favor
    or in which he is acquitted.
