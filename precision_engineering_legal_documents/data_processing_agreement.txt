RECITALS

WHEREAS:

A. The Parties have entered into [describe the main agreement] ("Principal
   Agreement") under which the Processor provides certain services to the
   Controller;

B. In the course of providing such services, the Processor may process Personal
   Data on behalf of the Controller;

C. The Parties wish to establish the terms governing such processing in
   compliance with applicable data protection laws, including the Digital
   Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDPA) and the Information Technology
   Act, 2000.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the
Parties agree as follows:

================================================================================

ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Applicable Laws" means the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023,
    Information Technology Act, 2000, Information Technology (Reasonable
    Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information)
    Rules, 2011, and any other applicable data protection laws.

1.2 "Data Breach" means any accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration,
    unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

1.3 "Data Principal" means the individual to whom the Personal Data relates.

1.4 "Data Subject Request" means a request from a Data Principal to exercise
    their rights under Applicable Laws.

1.5 "Personal Data" means any data about an individual who is identifiable by
    or in relation to such data ("Data Principal").

1.6 "Processing" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including
    collection, recording, organization, storage, adaptation, retrieval,
    consultation, use, disclosure, transmission, erasure, or destruction.

1.7 "Sensitive Personal Data" means Personal Data revealing financial data,
    health data, biometric data, genetic data, or any other data specified
    under Applicable Laws.

1.8 "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by the Processor to process
    Personal Data on behalf of the Controller.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 2: SCOPE AND PURPOSE

2.1 SCOPE

    This DPA applies to all Processing of Personal Data by the Processor on
    behalf of the Controller under the Principal Agreement.

2.2 PURPOSE

    The Processor shall process Personal Data only for the purposes specified
    in Annexure A and as necessary to provide the services under the Principal
    Agreement.

2.3 CATEGORIES OF DATA

    The categories of Personal Data and Data Principals are set out in Annexure A.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF THE PROCESSOR

3.1 GENERAL OBLIGATIONS

    The Processor shall:

    (a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller;
    (b) Ensure that persons authorized to process Personal Data are bound by
        confidentiality obligations;
    (c) Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures;
    (d) Not engage Sub-processors without prior written consent of the Controller;
    (e) Assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject Requests;
    (f) Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with Applicable Laws;
    (g) Delete or return all Personal Data upon termination of services;
    (h) Provide the Controller with all information necessary to demonstrate
        compliance.

3.2 SECURITY MEASURES

    The Processor shall implement appropriate security measures including:

    (a) Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest;
    (b) Access controls and authentication mechanisms;
    (c) Regular security assessments and penetration testing;
    (d) Backup and disaster recovery procedures;
    (e) Employee training on data protection;
    (f) Physical security of data processing facilities;
    (g) Logging and monitoring of access to Personal Data.

3.3 DATA LOCALIZATION

    The Processor shall store and process Personal Data within India unless
    otherwise permitted under Applicable Laws or with prior written consent
    of the Controller.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 4: SUB-PROCESSING

4.1 The Processor shall not engage any Sub-processor without prior written
    consent of the Controller.

4.2 Where the Controller provides general consent, the Processor shall inform
    the Controller of any intended changes and allow the Controller to object.

4.3 The Processor shall enter into a written agreement with each Sub-processor
    imposing obligations no less onerous than this DPA.

4.4 The Processor shall remain fully liable for the acts and omissions of its
    Sub-processors.

4.5 Current Sub-processors are listed in Annexure B.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 5: DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

5.1 The Processor shall promptly notify the Controller of any Data Subject
    Request received.

5.2 The Processor shall not respond to a Data Subject Request without the
    Controller's prior authorization, unless required by law.

5.3 The Processor shall assist the Controller in responding to Data Subject
    Requests, including requests for:

    (a) Access to Personal Data;
    (b) Correction of inaccurate Personal Data;
    (c) Erasure of Personal Data;
    (d) Restriction of Processing;
    (e) Data portability;
    (f) Information about Processing.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 6: DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

6.1 The Processor shall notify the Controller of any Data Breach without undue
    delay and in any event within 24 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

6.2 The notification shall include:

    (a) Description of the nature of the Data Breach;
    (b) Categories and approximate number of Data Principals affected;
    (c) Categories and approximate number of records affected;
    (d) Contact details of the Processor's data protection officer or contact
        point;
    (e) Description of likely consequences of the Data Breach;
    (f) Description of measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

6.3 The Processor shall cooperate with the Controller in investigating and
    remediating the Data Breach.

6.4 The Processor shall not communicate about the Data Breach to any third
    party without the Controller's prior approval, unless required by law.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 7: AUDIT AND COMPLIANCE

7.1 The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information
    necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

7.2 The Processor shall allow for and contribute to audits and inspections
    conducted by the Controller or an auditor appointed by the Controller.

7.3 The Processor shall provide the Controller with:

    (a) Copies of relevant certifications (ISO 27001, SOC 2, etc.);
    (b) Results of security assessments and penetration tests;
    (c) Audit reports from independent auditors;
    (d) Other documentation as reasonably requested.

7.4 Audits shall be conducted with reasonable notice and during normal business
    hours, subject to confidentiality obligations.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 8: DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

8.1 The Processor shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary for
    the purposes of Processing.

8.2 Upon termination of the Principal Agreement or upon Controller's request,
    the Processor shall:

    (a) Return all Personal Data to the Controller; or
    (b) Delete all Personal Data and certify such deletion in writing.

8.3 The Processor may retain Personal Data where required by Applicable Laws,
    subject to continued confidentiality and security obligations.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 9: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

9.1 The Processor shall be liable for any damages caused by Processing that
    violates this DPA or Applicable Laws.

9.2 The Processor shall indemnify the Controller against any claims, damages,
    fines, or expenses arising from:

    (a) Breach of this DPA by the Processor;
    (b) Non-compliance with Applicable Laws by the Processor;
    (c) Processing outside the scope of Controller's instructions;
    (d) Data Breaches caused by the Processor.

9.3 Liability limitations in the Principal Agreement shall not apply to
    breaches of this DPA.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 10: TERM AND TERMINATION

10.1 This DPA shall be effective from the Effective Date and shall continue
     for the duration of the Principal Agreement.

10.2 Termination of the Principal Agreement shall automatically terminate this
     DPA.

10.3 The obligations regarding data deletion, confidentiality, and audit shall
     survive termination.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 11: GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 GOVERNING LAW

     This DPA shall be governed by the laws of India.

11.2 DISPUTE RESOLUTION

     Any dispute shall be resolved through arbitration in Chennai under the
     Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

11.3 ENTIRE AGREEMENT

     This DPA, together with the Principal Agreement, constitutes the entire
     agreement regarding data processing.

11.4 AMENDMENT

     This DPA may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

11.5 SEVERABILITY

     If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue
     in full force.

11.6 CONFLICT

     In case of conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, this DPA
     shall prevail with respect to data processing matters.
