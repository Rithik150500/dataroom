                    GENERAL POWER OF ATTORNEY

================================================================================

KNOW ALL MEN BY THESE PRESENTS THAT:

PRECISION ENGINEERING WORKS PRIVATE LIMITED, a company incorporated under the
Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at Plot No. 45, SIPCOT
Industrial Estate, Hosur - 635126, Tamil Nadu (CIN: U28999TN2018PTC125847)
(hereinafter referred to as the "Principal" or "Company")

HEREBY APPOINTS AND CONSTITUTES:

MR. VIKRAM RAJAN, S/o Mr. Rajan Krishnan, aged about 38 years, residing at
No. 12, Anna Nagar East, Chennai - 600102, Tamil Nadu
(hereinafter referred to as the "Attorney")

as its true and lawful Attorney to do and execute all or any of the following
acts, deeds, and things on behalf of the Company:

================================================================================

ARTICLE 1: GENERAL POWERS

1.1 To act generally as the representative of the Company in all matters
    relating to its business.

1.2 To sign, execute, and deliver all documents, agreements, contracts, and
    instruments on behalf of the Company.

1.3 To appear before any Government authority, regulatory body, court, or
    tribunal and represent the Company.

1.4 To open, operate, and close bank accounts in the name of the Company.

1.5 To receive and issue receipts for any money, securities, or property due
    to the Company.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 2: CORPORATE MATTERS

2.1 To represent the Company before the Registrar of Companies and sign, file,
    and verify all forms, returns, and documents required under the Companies
    Act, 2013.

2.2 To apply for and obtain any licenses, registrations, permits, or approvals
    required for the business of the Company.

2.3 To attend and vote at general meetings of other companies on behalf of the
    Company.

2.4 To appoint and authorize representatives to represent the Company before
    any authority.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 3: TAX AND REGULATORY MATTERS

3.1 To represent the Company before any tax authority including the Income Tax
    Department, GST authorities, and other revenue authorities.

3.2 To sign and file tax returns, applications, appeals, and other documents
    on behalf of the Company.

3.3 To appear before the Customs, Central Excise, and Service Tax authorities.

3.4 To apply for and obtain PAN, TAN, GSTIN, IEC, and other registrations.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 4: BANKING AND FINANCIAL MATTERS

4.1 To open, operate, and close bank accounts in the name of the Company.

4.2 To sign cheques, demand drafts, and other negotiable instruments.

4.3 To avail loans, overdrafts, and credit facilities on behalf of the Company.

4.4 To create security, mortgage, hypothecation, or charge on the assets of
    the Company.

4.5 To invest surplus funds and manage the treasury operations of the Company.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 5: PROPERTY MATTERS

5.1 To purchase, sell, lease, mortgage, or otherwise deal with immovable
    property of the Company.

5.2 To execute sale deeds, lease agreements, mortgage deeds, and other
    documents relating to property.

5.3 To appear before the Sub-Registrar's office for registration of documents.

5.4 To pay stamp duty, registration fees, and other charges.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 6: LEGAL MATTERS

6.1 To institute, prosecute, defend, compromise, or settle any legal proceedings
    on behalf of the Company.

6.2 To appoint advocates, solicitors, and legal consultants to represent the
    Company.

6.3 To sign vakalatnamas, applications, affidavits, and other legal documents.

6.4 To appear before any court, tribunal, or arbitration proceedings.

6.5 To execute settlement agreements, consent decrees, and awards.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 7: EMPLOYMENT MATTERS

7.1 To appoint, promote, transfer, terminate, or discipline employees of the
    Company.

7.2 To sign employment agreements, appointment letters, and termination letters.

7.3 To represent the Company before labour authorities and industrial tribunals.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 8: COMMERCIAL MATTERS

8.1 To negotiate and execute contracts with customers, vendors, and partners.

8.2 To issue purchase orders, work orders, and service agreements.

8.3 To bid for tenders and participate in auctions on behalf of the Company.

8.4 To grant and receive licenses for intellectual property.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 9: DELEGATION

9.1 The Attorney may delegate any of the powers hereby granted to any other
    person by a written instrument, subject to the approval of the Board.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 10: RATIFICATION

10.1 The Company hereby agrees to ratify and confirm all acts done by the
     Attorney pursuant to this Power of Attorney.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 11: VALIDITY

11.1 This Power of Attorney shall come into force on the date of execution and
     shall remain in force until revoked by the Company.

11.2 The Company may revoke this Power of Attorney at any time by giving
     written notice to the Attorney.
