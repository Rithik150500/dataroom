2. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES

================================================================================

2.1 EQUAL OPPORTUNITY

We are an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race,
caste, religion, gender, age, disability, or any other protected characteristic.

2.2 RECRUITMENT

All recruitment shall be based on merit, qualifications, experience, and job
requirements. Background verification shall be conducted for all new hires.

2.3 PROBATION

- All new employees shall be on probation for six (6) months
- Probation may be extended based on performance
- Confirmation is subject to satisfactory completion of probation

2.4 EMPLOYMENT RECORDS

Employees must maintain updated personal information. Changes in address, phone
number, emergency contacts, etc., must be reported to HR within 7 days.

2.5 IDENTITY CARDS

All employees must wear Company identity cards while on premises. Lost cards
must be reported immediately.

3. CODE OF CONDUCT

================================================================================

3.1 PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR

All employees are expected to:
- Maintain professional conduct at all times
- Treat colleagues, customers, and vendors with respect
- Follow instructions from supervisors
- Complete assigned tasks diligently
- Maintain cleanliness and order in the workplace

3.2 PROHIBITED CONDUCT

The following are strictly prohibited:
- Harassment of any kind (sexual, verbal, physical)
- Discrimination
- Theft or misappropriation
- Intoxication or substance abuse
- Violence or threats
- Insubordination
- Falsification of records
- Unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Use of Company resources for personal gain

3.3 CONFLICT OF INTEREST

Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with Company
interests. Any potential conflict must be disclosed to management.

3.4 GIFTS AND ENTERTAINMENT

Employees shall not accept gifts or entertainment from vendors or customers
that may influence business decisions. Gifts exceeding Rs. 2,000 in value
must be reported to management.

4. WORKING HOURS AND ATTENDANCE

================================================================================

4.1 WORKING HOURS

- Regular working hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM
- Work days: Monday to Saturday
- Lunch break: 12:30 PM to 1:00 PM (30 minutes)
- Total working hours: 48 hours per week

4.2 SHIFT TIMINGS (PRODUCTION STAFF)

Shift A: 6:00 AM to 2:00 PM
Shift B: 2:00 PM to 10:00 PM
Shift C: 10:00 PM to 6:00 AM (Night shift)

Shift assignments are rotated weekly.

4.3 ATTENDANCE

- Employees must punch in/out using the biometric system
- Late arrival beyond 15 minutes requires prior approval
- Three instances of late arrival in a month may result in disciplinary action
- Unauthorized absence is a serious offense

4.4 OVERTIME

- Overtime work requires prior approval from supervisor
- Overtime is compensated at 2x the normal hourly rate
- Overtime hours are limited as per labor laws

5. LEAVE POLICIES

================================================================================

5.1 TYPES OF LEAVE

A. Earned Leave (EL)
   - 21 days per calendar year
   - Accrued at 1.75 days per month
   - Encashment allowed up to 50% at year end
   - Carry forward allowed up to 30 days

B. Sick Leave (SL)
   - 7 days per calendar year
   - Medical certificate required for more than 2 consecutive days
   - No encashment; lapses at year end

C. Casual Leave (CL)
   - 7 days per calendar year
   - Maximum 3 consecutive days
   - No encashment; lapses at year end

D. Maternity Leave
   - 26 weeks as per the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961
   - Applicable to women employees with less than 2 surviving children

E. Paternity Leave
   - 5 days within one month of child's birth

F. Bereavement Leave
   - 3 days for death of immediate family member

5.2 LEAVE APPLICATION

- Leave must be applied in advance through the HR system
- Emergency leave must be informed to supervisor immediately
- Unauthorized absence will be treated as Leave Without Pay (LWP)

5.3 PUBLIC HOLIDAYS

The Company observes 12 public holidays per year as per the holiday calendar
announced annually.

6. COMPENSATION AND BENEFITS

================================================================================

6.1 SALARY STRUCTURE

- Basic Salary: 40-50% of gross
- House Rent Allowance (HRA): 20% of basic
- Conveyance Allowance
- Special Allowance
- Other allowances as applicable

6.2 STATUTORY BENEFITS

A. Provident Fund (PF)
   - Employee contribution: 12% of basic
   - Employer contribution: 12% of basic

B. Employee State Insurance (ESI)

C. Gratuity
   - As per Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972

6.3 OTHER BENEFITS

- Group medical insurance for employee and family
- Personal accident insurance
- Free meals / canteen subsidy
- Uniform and safety shoes for production staff
- Transportation assistance

6.4 SALARY PAYMENT

- Salary is credited by the 7th of following month
- Payslips are available on the HR portal

9. HEALTH AND SAFETY

================================================================================

9.1 SAFETY POLICY

Safety is our top priority. All employees must follow safety guidelines and
report unsafe conditions immediately.

9.2 PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT (PPE)

The following PPE must be worn in designated areas:
- Safety shoes (steel-toed)
- Safety glasses
- Ear protection
- Gloves
- Hard hats (where required)

9.3 SAFETY RULES

- No loose clothing near machinery
- Long hair must be tied back
- No jewelry that could get caught
- No mobile phones on shop floor
- Follow lockout/tagout procedures
- Report all accidents and near-misses

9.4 EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

- Know the location of fire extinguishers and exits
- Follow evacuation procedures during drills and emergencies
- First aid kits are available in designated areas

9.5 HEALTH CHECK-UPS

- Pre-employment medical examination
- Annual health check-up
- Specific tests for employees in hazardous areas

10. DISCIPLINE AND GRIEVANCES

================================================================================

10.1 DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURE

Minor Offenses:
- First instance: Verbal warning
- Second instance: Written warning
- Third instance: Final warning and suspension

Major Offenses:
- May result in immediate suspension or termination

10.2 GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE

Step 1: Discuss with immediate supervisor
Step 2: Escalate to Department Head
Step 3: Approach HR Department
Step 4: Appeal to Management

All grievances shall be addressed within 15 working days.

10.3 ANTI-HARASSMENT POLICY

We have zero tolerance for harassment. The Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
handles all complaints under the Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act.

11. SEPARATION FROM EMPLOYMENT

================================================================================

11.1 RESIGNATION

- Submit resignation in writing to supervisor
- Serve notice period (30 days for confirmed employees)
- Complete exit formalities and handover

11.2 TERMINATION

The Company may terminate employment for:
- Poor performance
- Misconduct
- Redundancy
- Any other valid reason

11.3 EXIT FORMALITIES

- Exit interview
- Return of Company property

13. MANUFACTURING FLOOR POLICIES

================================================================================

13.1 5S WORKPLACE ORGANIZATION

- Sort: Remove unnecessary items
- Set in Order: Organize everything
- Shine: Clean the workplace
- Standardize: Create standards
- Sustain: Maintain discipline

13.2 MACHINE OPERATION

- Only trained operators may operate machines
- Follow startup and shutdown procedures
- Report any machine malfunction immediately
- Perform daily maintenance checks

13.3 TOOL MANAGEMENT

- Sign out tools from tool crib
- Return tools after use
- Report damaged or worn tools
- Handle precision tools with care

13.4 MATERIAL HANDLING

- Use proper lifting techniques
- Use forklifts and cranes for heavy items
- Stack materials properly
- Follow FIFO (First In, First Out)
