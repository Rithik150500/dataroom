================================================================================

ARTICLE 1: APPOINTMENT

1.1 The Company hereby appoints the Employee, and the Employee hereby accepts
    the appointment, as [Designation] in the [Department] of the Company.

1.2 The Employee shall report to [_______________] or such other person as may
    be designated by the Company from time to time.

1.3 The place of work shall be at the Company's facility at Plot No. 45, SIPCOT
    Industrial Estate, Hosur - 635126, Tamil Nadu, or such other location as
    may be assigned by the Company.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 2: COMMENCEMENT AND TERM

2.1 This employment shall commence on [_______________] ("Commencement Date").

2.2 The Employee shall be on probation for a period of six (6) months from the
    Commencement Date.

2.3 Upon successful completion of the probation period and confirmation by the
    Company, the employment shall be deemed to be confirmed.

2.4 This employment shall continue until terminated in accordance with the
    provisions of this Agreement.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 3: DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

3.1 The Employee shall perform such duties and responsibilities as are assigned
    from time to time, commensurate with the position held.

3.2 The Employee shall:

    (a) Devote full time, attention, and abilities to the Company's business;
    (b) Faithfully and diligently perform all duties assigned;
    (c) Obey all lawful instructions of the management;
    (d) Act in the best interests of the Company at all times;
    (e) Not engage in any other employment or business without prior written
        consent of the Company;
    (f) Comply with all Company policies, procedures, and Code of Conduct;
    (g) Maintain high standards of quality and workmanship;
    (h) Follow all safety protocols and use protective equipment as required.

3.3 The specific duties and responsibilities are set out in ANNEXURE A.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 4: WORKING HOURS

4.1 The normal working hours shall be from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday to
    Saturday, with a lunch break of 30 minutes.

4.2 The Employee may be required to work beyond normal working hours,
    including on Sundays and holidays, based on operational requirements.

4.3 Overtime shall be compensated as per Company policy and applicable laws.

4.4 The Employee shall maintain attendance as per Company procedures and
    record work hours accurately.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 5: COMPENSATION

5.1 The Employee shall be entitled to a gross annual compensation of
    Rs. [_______________] (Rupees [_______________] Only).

5.2 The compensation structure is set out in ANNEXURE B.

5.3 All payments shall be subject to deduction of applicable taxes, provident
    fund contributions, and other statutory deductions.

5.4 Salary shall be paid on or before the 7th day of the following month.

5.5 The compensation shall be reviewed annually based on performance and
    Company policy.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 6: BENEFITS

6.1 The Employee shall be entitled to the following benefits:

    (a) Provident Fund as per the Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous
        Provisions Act, 1952;
    (b) ESI (Employee State Insurance) as applicable;
    (c) Gratuity as per the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972;
    (d) Annual leave of [21] days per calendar year;
    (e) Sick leave of [7] days per calendar year;
    (f) Public holidays as per Company calendar;
    (g) Medical insurance as per Company policy;
    (h) Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as required for the job.

6.2 Leave accumulation, encashment, and carry-forward shall be as per Company
    policy.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 7: CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 The Employee shall maintain strict confidentiality of all proprietary and
    confidential information of the Company, including but not limited to:

    (a) Technical data, drawings, designs, and specifications;
    (b) Manufacturing processes and procedures;
    (c) Customer lists, pricing, and terms;
    (d) Business strategies and plans;
    (e) Financial information;
    (f) Employee information;
    (g) Trade secrets and know-how.

7.2 The Employee shall not disclose confidential information to any third party
    without prior written consent of the Company.

7.3 Upon termination, the Employee shall return all confidential information
    and materials in any form.

7.4 This confidentiality obligation shall survive termination of this Agreement
    for a period of five (5) years.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 8: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1 All inventions, designs, processes, improvements, and other intellectual
    property created by the Employee during employment shall be the sole
    property of the Company.

8.2 The Employee hereby assigns all rights in such intellectual property to the
    Company and agrees to execute all documents necessary to perfect such
    assignment.

8.3 The Employee shall promptly disclose all inventions and discoveries to the
    Company.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 9: NON-COMPETE AND NON-SOLICITATION

9.1 During employment and for a period of one (1) year after termination, the
    Employee shall not:

    (a) Directly or indirectly engage in any business competitive with the
        Company's business within India;
    (b) Solicit or attempt to solicit any customer or client of the Company;
    (c) Induce or attempt to induce any employee of the Company to leave;
    (d) Interfere with the Company's relationships with vendors and suppliers.

9.2 The Employee acknowledges that these restrictions are reasonable and
    necessary to protect the Company's legitimate business interests.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 10: TERMINATION

10.1 TERMINATION BY COMPANY

     The Company may terminate this Agreement:

     (a) During probation: By giving 7 days' notice or payment in lieu;
     (b) After confirmation: By giving 30 days' notice or payment in lieu;
     (c) Without notice for cause, including:
         - Misconduct or insubordination
         - Fraud, dishonesty, or theft
         - Negligence causing loss to Company
         - Violation of Company policies
         - Breach of confidentiality
         - Conviction for a criminal offense
         - Failure to perform duties satisfactorily

10.2 TERMINATION BY EMPLOYEE

     The Employee may terminate this Agreement:

     (a) During probation: By giving 15 days' notice;
     (b) After confirmation: By giving 30 days' notice.

10.3 The Company may, at its discretion, waive the notice period in whole or
     in part.

10.4 Upon termination, the Employee shall:

     (a) Complete all pending work and hand over responsibilities;
     (b) Return all Company property, documents, and materials;
     (c) Clear all dues and advances;
     (d) Obtain a no-dues certificate.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 11: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

11.1 The Employee represents and warrants that:

     (a) All information provided to the Company is true and accurate;
     (b) The Employee is legally entitled to work in India;
     (c) The Employee is not bound by any agreement that would prevent
         performance of duties;
     (d) The Employee is in good health and fit for employment;
     (e) The Employee has no criminal convictions.

11.2 Any misrepresentation shall be grounds for immediate termination.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 12: INDEMNIFICATION

12.1 The Employee shall indemnify and hold harmless the Company against any
     loss, damage, or liability arising from:

     (a) Breach of this Agreement by the Employee;
     (b) Negligence or misconduct of the Employee;
     (c) Violation of any law by the Employee.

================================================================================

ARTICLE 13: GENERAL PROVISIONS

13.1 ENTIRE AGREEMENT
     This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and
     supersedes all prior agreements.

13.2 AMENDMENT
     This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both
     Parties.

13.3 WAIVER
     No waiver of any provision shall be deemed a waiver of any other provision
     or a continuing waiver.

13.4 SEVERABILITY
     If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall continue
     in full force and effect.

13.5 GOVERNING LAW
     This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India.

13.6 DISPUTE RESOLUTION
     Any dispute shall be resolved through arbitration in Chennai under the
     Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996.

13.7 NOTICES
     All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses specified
     herein.
