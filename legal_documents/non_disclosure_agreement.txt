          ARTICLE 2: EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

2.1 Confidential Information does not include information that:

    (a) Was already in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or
        becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party;

    (b) Was rightfully in the possession of the Receiving Party prior to
        disclosure, as evidenced by written records;

    (c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of
        or reference to the Confidential Information;

    (d) Is rightfully received from a third party without restriction on
        disclosure and without breach of this Agreement;

    (e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order,
        provided that the Receiving Party gives prompt notice to the
        Disclosing Party to enable it to seek a protective order.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

3.1 NON-DISCLOSURE
    The Receiving Party agrees to:
    (a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence;
    (b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without
        prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;
    (c) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose.

3.2 STANDARD OF CARE
    The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information using
    the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential
    information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

3.3 LIMITED DISCLOSURE
    The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to its
    employees, officers, directors, advisors, and contractors who:
    (a) Have a need to know for the Purpose;
    (b) Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective
        as this Agreement;
    (c) Are informed of the confidential nature of the information.

3.4 RESPONSIBILITY
    The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this
    Agreement by any person to whom it has disclosed Confidential
    Information.

================================================================================

           ARTICLE 4: NO LICENSE OR OWNERSHIP

4.1 NO LICENSE
    Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any license or
    right to use any Confidential Information, except for the Purpose.

4.2 NO OWNERSHIP
    All Confidential Information shall remain the sole property of the
    Disclosing Party. No right, title, or interest in any intellectual
    property is transferred or granted by this Agreement.

4.3 NO OBLIGATION
    Nothing in this Agreement obligates either Party to:
    (a) Enter into any further agreement;
    (b) Disclose any particular Confidential Information;
    (c) Purchase any products or services;
    (d) Continue discussions regarding the Purpose.

================================================================================

                ARTICLE 5: RETURN OF INFORMATION

5.1 RETURN OR DESTRUCTION
    Upon the written request of the Disclosing Party, or upon termination
    of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:
    (a) Return all Confidential Information in tangible form;
    (b) Destroy all copies, extracts, and derivatives of Confidential
        Information in any form;
    (c) Provide written certification of such return or destruction.

5.2 EXCEPTIONS
    The Receiving Party may retain:
    (a) One copy for archival purposes in a secure location;
    (b) Copies required by law or regulation;
    (c) Backup copies on IT systems, subject to continued confidentiality.

================================================================================

                    ARTICLE 6: TERM

6.1 TERM OF AGREEMENT
    This Agreement shall be effective from the date first written above
    and shall continue for a period of [2/3] years, unless terminated
    earlier by either Party with 30 days' written notice.

6.2 SURVIVAL
    The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive
    termination and continue for a period of [3/5] years from the date
    of disclosure of the Confidential Information, or for such longer
    period as the Confidential Information qualifies as a trade secret.
