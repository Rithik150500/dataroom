                    SECTION 2: WHAT ARE COOKIES
================================================================================

2.1 DEFINITION

    Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer,
    smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to
    make websites work efficiently and provide information to site owners.

2.2 HOW COOKIES WORK

    (a) When you visit our Platform, a cookie is downloaded to your device;
    (b) The cookie stores information about your visit;
    (c) When you return, the cookie is retrieved;
    (d) This allows the Platform to "remember" your preferences and actions.

2.3 COOKIE CONTENTS

    Cookies typically contain:

    (a) Name of the cookie;
    (b) Unique identifier;
    (c) Website domain;
    (d) Expiration date;
    (e) Stored data (preferences, session info, etc.).

2.4 SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

    In addition to cookies, we may use:

    (a) WEB BEACONS: Small graphic images that track user behavior
    (b) PIXELS: Tiny images in emails to track opens and clicks
    (c) LOCAL STORAGE: Data stored in your browser
    (d) SESSION STORAGE: Temporary data for single session
    (e) FINGERPRINTING: Device identification techniques

    This policy covers all such technologies.

================================================================================
                    SECTION 4: TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE
================================================================================

4.1 BY DURATION

    (a) SESSION COOKIES
        - Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
        - Used for essential functions like shopping cart
        - Do not collect information from your device

    (b) PERSISTENT COOKIES
        - Remain on your device for a set period or until deleted
        - Used for preferences, analytics, and advertising
        - Help recognize returning users

4.2 BY SOURCE

    (a) FIRST-PARTY COOKIES
        - Set by TechVenture directly
        - Essential for Platform operation
        - Subject to our privacy practices

    (b) THIRD-PARTY COOKIES
        - Set by our partners and service providers
        - Used for analytics, advertising, and functionality
        - Subject to third-party privacy policies

4.3 BY PURPOSE

    (a) STRICTLY NECESSARY COOKIES
        - Essential for website operation
        - Cannot be disabled
        - Examples: Authentication, security, cart management

    (b) PERFORMANCE COOKIES
        - Collect information about Platform usage
        - Help us improve our services
        - Examples: Analytics, error tracking

    (c) FUNCTIONAL COOKIES
        - Enable enhanced features and personalization
        - Remember user preferences
        - Examples: Language, location, display settings

    (d) TARGETING/ADVERTISING COOKIES
        - Track browsing activity across websites
        - Deliver relevant advertisements
        - Examples: Retargeting, ad measurement

                    SECTION 8: MANAGING COOKIES BY BROWSER
================================================================================

8.1 BROWSER SETTINGS

    You can control cookies through browser settings:

    (a) GOOGLE CHROME
        Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
        Or: chrome://settings/cookies

    (b) MOZILLA FIREFOX
        Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
        Or: about:preferences#privacy

    (c) SAFARI
        Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
        Or: Safari > Preferences > Privacy

    (d) MICROSOFT EDGE
        Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies
        Or: edge://settings/content/cookies

    (e) OPERA
        Settings > Advanced > Privacy & Security > Cookies

8.2 MOBILE BROWSERS

    (a) SAFARI (iOS)
        Settings > Safari > Privacy & Security

    (b) CHROME (Android)
        Menu > Settings > Privacy > Cookies

8.3 BROWSER ACTIONS

    You can:

    (a) Block all cookies;
    (b) Block third-party cookies only;
    (c) Delete existing cookies;
    (d) Allow specific websites;
    (e) Set up automatic deletion.

8.4 PRIVATE BROWSING

    (a) Use Incognito/Private mode;
    (b) Cookies deleted when window closes;
    (c) No persistent tracking;
    (d) Recommended for shared devices.

                    SECTION 10: IMPACT OF DISABLING COOKIES
================================================================================

10.1 GENERAL IMPACT

    Disabling cookies may:

    (a) Affect website functionality;
    (b) Prevent saving preferences;
    (c) Require repeated login;
    (d) Show less relevant content;
    (e) Impact shopping cart functionality.

10.2 SPECIFIC IMPACTS

    (a) STRICTLY NECESSARY DISABLED
        - Cannot process orders
        - Login not possible
        - Cart not functional
        - Platform unusable

    (b) PERFORMANCE DISABLED
        - We cannot improve services
        - Issues may go undetected
        - No impact on user experience

    (c) FUNCTIONAL DISABLED
        - Preferences not remembered
        - Repeated configuration needed
        - Recently viewed not available

    (d) ADVERTISING DISABLED
        - See generic/random ads
        - Ads may not be relevant
        - More total ads may be shown

10.3 RECOMMENDATION

    We recommend keeping at least "Strictly Necessary" and "Functional"
    cookies enabled for the best experience on our Platform.
