                          RECITALS

WHEREAS:

A. The Assignor is [a founder / an employee / a contractor] of the Company.

B. In connection with the Assignor's relationship with the Company, the
   Assignor has created, developed, or contributed to certain intellectual
   property that is related to or useful for the business of the Company.

C. The Parties wish to formalize the assignment of such intellectual property
   to the Company on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements
contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt
and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

================================================================================

                  ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Assigned IP" means:
    (a) All Prior IP as described in Schedule A;
    (b) All Work Product created by the Assignor;
    (c) All improvements, modifications, and derivatives thereof.

1.2 "Company Business" means the business of developing and providing
    software as a service (SaaS) solutions, enterprise software, and
    related services.

1.3 "Confidential Information" means any proprietary or confidential
    information of the Company.

1.4 "Intellectual Property Rights" means all rights, title, and interest
    in and to:
    (a) Patents and patent applications;
    (b) Copyrights and copyrightable works;
    (c) Trademarks, service marks, and trade names;
    (d) Trade secrets and know-how;
    (e) Software, source code, and object code;
    (f) Domain names and URLs;
    (g) All other intellectual property rights.

1.5 "Prior IP" means intellectual property created by the Assignor prior
    to joining the Company that is related to or useful for the Company
    Business, as listed in Schedule A.

1.6 "Work Product" means all inventions, discoveries, developments, designs,
    ideas, concepts, software, code, documentation, and other work created
    by the Assignor:
    (a) During the term of employment/engagement;
    (b) In connection with or relating to the Company Business;
    (c) Using Company resources, facilities, or information.

================================================================================

                  ARTICLE 2: ASSIGNMENT OF IP

2.1 ASSIGNMENT OF PRIOR IP
    The Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the
    Company all right, title, and interest in and to the Prior IP listed in
    Schedule A, including all Intellectual Property Rights therein.

2.2 ASSIGNMENT OF WORK PRODUCT
    The Assignor hereby irrevocably assigns, transfers, and conveys to the
    Company all right, title, and interest in and to all Work Product,
    including all Intellectual Property Rights therein.

2.3 FUTURE WORK PRODUCT
    The Assignor agrees that all Work Product created during the term of
    employment/engagement shall automatically be assigned to the Company
    upon creation.

2.4 WORK FOR HIRE
    To the extent any Work Product qualifies as "work for hire" under
    applicable law, such Work Product shall be considered work made for
    hire for the Company.

2.5 NO RETAINED RIGHTS
    The Assignor retains no rights in the Assigned IP except as expressly
    granted in writing by the Company.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 3: CONSIDERATION

3.1 FOR EMPLOYEES/FOUNDERS
    The consideration for this assignment includes:
    (a) The Assignor's employment/engagement with the Company;
    (b) Salary, compensation, and benefits;
    (c) Stock options or equity grants (if applicable);
    (d) Access to Company resources and opportunities.

3.2 FOR CONTRACTORS
    The consideration for this assignment is included in the fees payable
    under the Assignor's service agreement with the Company.

3.3 ACKNOWLEDGMENT
    The Assignor acknowledges that the consideration is adequate and
    sufficient for this assignment.

================================================================================

              ARTICLE 4: REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

4.1 ASSIGNOR REPRESENTATIONS
    The Assignor represents and warrants that:

    (a) OWNERSHIP: The Assignor is the sole owner of the Assigned IP and
        has full right and authority to make this assignment.

    (b) NO ENCUMBRANCES: The Assigned IP is free and clear of all liens,
        encumbrances, security interests, and claims.

    (c) NO INFRINGEMENT: The Assigned IP does not infringe any third-party
        intellectual property rights.

    (d) NO PRIOR ASSIGNMENT: The Assignor has not previously assigned,
        transferred, or licensed any rights in the Assigned IP.

    (e) COMPLETE DISCLOSURE: Schedule A contains a complete and accurate
        list of all Prior IP.

    (f) NO THIRD-PARTY CLAIMS: There are no pending or threatened claims
        relating to the Assigned IP.

    (g) NO EMPLOYER RESTRICTIONS: The Assignor is not subject to any
        agreement with a prior employer that would prevent this assignment.

4.2 INDEMNIFICATION
    The Assignor shall indemnify and hold harmless the Company against
    any claims arising from breach of these representations and warranties.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 5: FURTHER ASSURANCES

5.1 COOPERATION
    The Assignor agrees to:

    (a) Execute any additional documents necessary to perfect the Company's
        rights in the Assigned IP, including patent applications,
        copyright registrations, and trademark applications;

    (b) Provide all information and materials relating to the Assigned IP;

    (c) Testify in any legal proceedings relating to the Assigned IP;

    (d) Assist in the prosecution or defense of any IP-related claims;

    (e) Maintain records of Work Product creation.

5.2 POWER OF ATTORNEY
    In the event the Assignor fails or refuses to execute any required
    documents, the Company is hereby irrevocably appointed as the
    Assignor's attorney-in-fact to execute such documents on behalf of
    the Assignor.

5.3 SURVIVAL
    The Assignor's obligations under this Article shall survive termination
    of employment/engagement.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 6: MORAL RIGHTS

6.1 WAIVER OF MORAL RIGHTS
    To the extent permitted by applicable law, the Assignor hereby:

    (a) Waives all moral rights in the Assigned IP, including the right
        of attribution and the right of integrity;

    (b) Consents to any action by the Company that would otherwise
        infringe such moral rights;

    (c) Agrees not to assert any moral rights against the Company or
        its licensees.

6.2 ATTRIBUTION
    The Company may, at its discretion, attribute authorship to the
    Assignor, but is under no obligation to do so.

================================================================================

              ARTICLE 7: CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION
    The Assignor agrees that:

    (a) The Assigned IP and all related materials are Confidential
        Information of the Company;

    (b) The Assignor shall maintain the confidentiality of such information;

    (c) The Assignor shall not use or disclose such information except
        as required for the Company's business.

7.2 RETURN OF MATERIALS
    Upon termination of employment/engagement, the Assignor shall return
    all materials relating to the Assigned IP.

================================================================================

            ARTICLE 8: EXCLUDED INVENTIONS

8.1 PRIOR INVENTIONS
    The inventions, if any, listed in Schedule B are excluded from this
    Agreement as they were created prior to the Assignor's relationship
    with the Company and are not related to the Company Business.

8.2 PERSONAL PROJECTS
    Inventions created entirely on the Assignor's own time, without use
    of Company resources, and not related to the Company Business are
    excluded, provided such inventions are disclosed to and approved by
    the Company in writing.

8.3 DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENT
    The Assignor shall promptly disclose to the Company any invention
    that may reasonably be considered to be related to the Company
    Business to allow the Company to determine ownership.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 9: GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 ENTIRE AGREEMENT
    This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties
    with respect to the subject matter hereof.

9.2 AMENDMENTS
    This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by
    both Parties.

9.3 SEVERABILITY
    If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall
    remain in full force and effect.

9.4 ASSIGNMENT
    This Agreement and the rights assigned hereunder may be assigned by
    the Company to any successor or affiliate without consent.

9.5 GOVERNING LAW
    This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of India. Any disputes
    shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of courts in Bangalore.

9.6 SURVIVAL
    The provisions of this Agreement shall survive termination of the
    Assignor's employment or engagement with the Company.
