                          RECITALS

WHEREAS:

A. The Principal is engaged in the business of developing and providing
   Software as a Service (SaaS) solutions for enterprise resource management.

B. The Principal requires an authorized representative to act on its behalf
   for the purposes specified herein.

C. The Principal is satisfied with the capacity, integrity, and ability of
   the Attorney to act in the best interests of the Company.

NOW, THEREFORE, the Principal hereby appoints the Attorney as its true and
lawful attorney to do and execute all or any of the following acts, deeds,
and things on behalf of and in the name of the Principal:

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 1: BANKING OPERATIONS

The Attorney is authorized to:

1.1 Operate and manage all bank accounts of the Company, including current
    accounts, savings accounts, and fixed deposits;

1.2 Open new bank accounts and close existing accounts;

1.3 Sign cheques, demand drafts, and payment instructions up to a limit of
    Rs. _______ (Rupees _______ Only) per transaction;

1.4 Authorize electronic fund transfers, NEFT, RTGS, and other digital
    payments;

1.5 Apply for and manage credit facilities, overdrafts, and loans;

1.6 Provide instructions to bankers regarding standing instructions and
    direct debits;

1.7 Collect and receive payments, refunds, and other amounts due to the
    Company;

1.8 Execute documents required by banks and financial institutions.

================================================================================

              ARTICLE 2: GOVERNMENT AUTHORITIES

The Attorney is authorized to:

2.1 Represent the Company before all government authorities including but
    not limited to:
    (a) Registrar of Companies (ROC)
    (b) Income Tax Department
    (c) Goods and Services Tax (GST) authorities
    (d) Provident Fund authorities
    (e) Employee State Insurance (ESI) authorities
    (f) Professional Tax authorities
    (g) Labour Commissioner and Labour Department
    (h) Shop & Establishment authorities
    (i) Municipal Corporation

2.2 File returns, forms, and applications with government authorities;

2.3 Appear and represent the Company in hearings, assessments, and proceedings;

2.4 Sign and submit declarations, undertakings, and affidavits;

2.5 Apply for and obtain licenses, permits, certificates, and approvals;

2.6 Pay taxes, fees, duties, penalties, and other government charges;

2.7 Receive notices, orders, and communications from authorities;

2.8 File appeals, objections, and representations;

2.9 Execute bonds, guarantees, and indemnities as required by authorities.

================================================================================

             ARTICLE 3: CONTRACTS AND AGREEMENTS

The Attorney is authorized to:

3.1 Negotiate, draft, and execute contracts, agreements, and memoranda of
    understanding with customers, vendors, and partners, subject to the
    following limits:
    (a) Contracts up to Rs. _______ (Rupees _______ Only) in value
    (b) Contracts for a term not exceeding _______ years

3.2 Modify, amend, and renew existing contracts within authorized limits;

3.3 Issue notices for termination or breach of contract;

3.4 Execute non-disclosure agreements and confidentiality agreements;

3.5 Sign service level agreements and operational documents;

3.6 Issue and accept purchase orders and work orders;

3.7 Execute lease agreements and license agreements for premises and equipment.

================================================================================

             ARTICLE 4: LEGAL PROCEEDINGS

The Attorney is authorized to:

4.1 Represent the Company in all civil and criminal proceedings, arbitrations,
    mediations, and other dispute resolution forums;

4.2 Engage and instruct advocates, solicitors, and legal counsel;

4.3 File suits, petitions, applications, complaints, and claims;

4.4 Defend suits, petitions, and proceedings filed against the Company;

4.5 Sign vakalatnamas, power of attorneys for legal counsel, and written
    statements;

4.6 Attend hearings, examinations, and proceedings;

4.7 Negotiate and execute settlements, compromises, and consent orders;

4.8 Withdraw cases and accept service of process;

4.9 Execute decrees, awards, and orders;

4.10 File and pursue appeals and revisions.

================================================================================

            ARTICLE 5: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

The Attorney is authorized to:

5.1 Apply for registration of trademarks, copyrights, patents, and designs;

5.2 File responses to examination reports and office actions;

5.3 Execute assignments, licenses, and other IP-related documents;

5.4 Renew registrations and pay renewal fees;

5.5 Oppose third-party applications and defend the Company's IP;

5.6 File complaints for IP infringement.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 6: EMPLOYMENT MATTERS

The Attorney is authorized to:

6.1 Issue offer letters and appointment letters;

6.2 Execute employment agreements and contractor agreements;

6.3 Issue salary revisions, promotions, and transfers;

6.4 Issue warning letters, show cause notices, and termination letters;

6.5 Represent the Company in labour proceedings and disputes;

6.6 Execute full and final settlements with employees.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 7: GENERAL POWERS

The Attorney is authorized to:

7.1 Correspond and communicate on behalf of the Company;

7.2 Sign applications, forms, and declarations;

7.3 Give undertakings and indemnities on behalf of the Company;

7.4 Collect documents, certificates, and property belonging to the Company;

7.5 Appoint sub-agents for specific tasks (subject to prior approval);

7.6 Do all such acts and things as may be necessary and incidental to the
    above powers.

================================================================================

               ARTICLE 8: LIMITATIONS

8.1 This Power of Attorney does NOT authorize the Attorney to:

    (a) Sell, mortgage, or otherwise dispose of immovable property;
    (b) Borrow money or create security over Company assets;
    (c) Approve related party transactions;
    (d) Change the authorized signatories of the Company;
    (e) Enter into agreements exceeding the specified monetary or time limits;
    (f) Take any action that requires Board or Shareholder approval under
        the Companies Act, 2013;
    (g) Delegate these powers to another person without prior approval.

8.2 All actions taken by the Attorney shall be in compliance with:
    (a) The Articles of Association of the Company;
    (b) Resolutions of the Board of Directors;
    (c) Applicable laws and regulations;
    (d) Any specific instructions issued by the Company.

================================================================================

          ARTICLE 9: VALIDITY AND REVOCATION

9.1 VALIDITY
    This Power of Attorney shall be valid for a period of [1/2/3] year(s)
    from the date of execution unless revoked earlier.

9.2 REVOCATION
    This Power of Attorney may be revoked by the Principal at any time by
    written notice to the Attorney and registration of revocation where
    required.

9.3 AUTOMATIC TERMINATION
    This Power of Attorney shall automatically terminate upon:
    (a) Expiry of the validity period;
    (b) Death, incapacity, or insolvency of the Attorney;
    (c) Resignation or removal of the Attorney from the Company;
    (d) Winding up or dissolution of the Company.

9.4 EFFECT OF TERMINATION
    Upon termination, the Attorney shall:
    (a) Cease to act under this Power of Attorney;
    (b) Return all documents and property to the Company;
    (c) Account for all actions taken under this Power of Attorney.

================================================================================

              ARTICLE 10: INDEMNIFICATION

10.1 The Company agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Attorney against
     all claims, losses, and expenses arising from actions taken in good
     faith under this Power of Attorney.

10.2 The Attorney shall be personally liable for actions taken in bad faith,
     fraud, gross negligence, or in excess of the authority granted herein.

================================================================================

              ARTICLE 11: GENERAL PROVISIONS

11.1 GOVERNING LAW
     This Power of Attorney shall be governed by the laws of India.

11.2 REGISTRATION
     This Power of Attorney shall be registered with the Sub-Registrar
     where required under the Registration Act, 1908.

11.3 RATIFICATION
     The Principal hereby ratifies all acts done by the Attorney in good
     faith prior to revocation of this Power of Attorney.

================================================================================
