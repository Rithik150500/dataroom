9. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS
======================

9.1 REGRESSION ANALYSIS (Assay vs Time)

Long-term (30°C/65% RH):
- Degradation rate: 0.25% per month
- Projected shelf life (90% lower confidence): 28 months
- R² = 0.998

Accelerated (40°C/75% RH):
- Degradation rate: 1.85% per month
- Significant degradation observed

9.2 DISSOLUTION TREND

Long-term: Slight decrease over 24 months but well within specification.
All time points: >80% (Specification: NLT 80%)

9.3 IMPURITY TREND

- Individual impurities increased from 0.3% to 1.8% over 24 months
- Total impurities increased from 0.8% to 3.5% over 24 months
- Well within specification limits throughout

================================================================================

10. DISCUSSION
=============

10.1 LONG-TERM STABILITY (30°C/65% RH)

All three batches remained within specification through 24 months of storage
at 30°C/65% RH. The observed trends are:

- Assay decreased from ~100% to ~94% (within 90-120% specification)
- Dissolution decreased from ~95% to ~90% (above 80% specification)
- Impurities increased as expected due to hydrolysis
- Water content increased slightly but remained within limits

The product demonstrates adequate stability for the intended 24-month shelf life.

10.2 ACCELERATED STABILITY (40°C/75% RH)

Under accelerated conditions:
- Assay dropped to ~88% at 6 months (below 90% specification)
- Dissolution dropped to ~82% at 6 months (still above 80% specification)
- Impurities increased significantly

This is expected for beta-lactam antibiotics which are susceptible to
hydrolysis under high temperature and humidity conditions.

10.3 PACKAGING PERFORMANCE

The Alu-Alu strip packaging provides adequate protection against moisture
and supports the 24-month shelf life at 30°C/65% RH.

================================================================================

11. CONCLUSION
=============

Based on the stability data obtained:

1. All three batches of Amoxicillin Capsules BP 500mg met all specifications
   throughout 24 months of long-term storage at 30°C ± 2°C / 65% ± 5% RH.

2. The product demonstrates adequate stability for the proposed shelf life.

3. Accelerated stability data shows expected degradation typical of
   beta-lactam antibiotics but does not indicate any unexpected instability.

RECOMMENDATION:

Shelf Life:           24 months from date of manufacture
Storage Condition:    Store below 30°C
                      Protect from moisture
                      Store in original pack
