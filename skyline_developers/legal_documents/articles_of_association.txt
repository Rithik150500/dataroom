================================================================================
                       PART I - PRELIMINARY
================================================================================

1. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

   (a) In these Articles, unless the context otherwise requires:

       "Act" means the Companies Act, 2013 or any statutory modification or
       re-enactment thereof for the time being in force.

       "Articles" means these Articles of Association of the Company.

       "Board" or "Board of Directors" means the collective body of Directors
       of the Company.

       "Company" means SKYLINE DEVELOPERS PRIVATE LIMITED.

       "Director" means a Director of the Company.

       "Memorandum" means the Memorandum of Association of the Company.

       "Seal" means the Common Seal of the Company.

       "Secretary" means the Company Secretary of the Company.

   (b) Words importing the singular number shall include the plural number
       and vice versa.

   (c) Words importing the masculine gender shall include the feminine gender.

2. TABLE F APPLICABILITY

   The regulations contained in Table F of Schedule I to the Act shall apply
   to the Company except to the extent that they are modified or excluded by
   these Articles.

================================================================================
            PART II - SHARE CAPITAL AND VARIATION OF RIGHTS
================================================================================

3. SHARE CAPITAL

   (a) The Authorized Share Capital of the Company is Rs. 25,00,00,000/-
       (Rupees Twenty-Five Crores Only) divided into 25,00,000 (Twenty-Five
       Lakh) Equity Shares of Rs. 100/- (Rupees One Hundred Only) each.

   (b) The minimum paid-up capital of the Company shall be such amount as
       may be prescribed under the Act from time to time.

4. INCREASE OF SHARE CAPITAL

   The Company may from time to time by ordinary resolution increase the
   share capital by such sum to be divided into shares of such amount as
   may be specified in the resolution.

5. REDUCTION OF SHARE CAPITAL

   Subject to the provisions of the Act, the Company may by special resolution
   reduce its share capital in any way.

6. VARIATION OF RIGHTS

   If at any time the share capital is divided into different classes of
   shares, the rights attached to any class may be varied with the consent
   in writing of the holders of not less than three-fourths of the issued
   shares of that class, or with the sanction of a special resolution.

================================================================================
              PART III - SHARES AND TRANSFER OF SHARES
================================================================================

7. RESTRICTION ON NUMBER OF MEMBERS

   The number of members of the Company (exclusive of its employees and
   former employees who acquired shares during employment) shall not exceed
   200 (Two Hundred).

8. RESTRICTION ON INVITATION TO PUBLIC

   The Company shall not invite the public to subscribe for any shares in
   or debentures of the Company.

9. RESTRICTION ON TRANSFER OF SHARES

   (a) The shares shall be freely transferable among the existing members
       of the Company.

   (b) No share shall be transferred to any person who is not a member of
       the Company so long as any member is willing to purchase the same at
       a fair value determined by the Board.

   (c) The Board may decline to register the transfer of shares to a person
       who is not a member, except in the case of transfer by will or
       intestate succession.

10. RIGHT OF FIRST REFUSAL

    (a) Any member desiring to transfer shares shall first offer the same
        to the existing members in proportion to their existing shareholding.

    (b) The offer shall be at a fair value to be determined by the Company's
        auditors.

    (c) The existing members shall have 30 (thirty) days to accept or reject
        the offer.

    (d) If the existing members do not purchase all the offered shares, the
        transferring member may transfer the remaining shares to any third
        party at a price not less than the fair value.

11. INSTRUMENT OF TRANSFER

    (a) The instrument of transfer of shares shall be in writing and shall
        be signed by or on behalf of both the transferor and the transferee.

    (b) The instrument of transfer shall be in Form SH-4 or such other form
        as may be prescribed.

    (c) The Company shall not register a transfer of shares unless a proper
        instrument of transfer duly stamped and executed is delivered to the
        Company.

12. CLOSURE OF REGISTER

    The Board may close the Register of Members for any period not exceeding
    45 (forty-five) days in any one year, but not exceeding 30 (thirty) days
    at a time.

================================================================================
                   PART IV - TRANSMISSION OF SHARES
================================================================================

13. TRANSMISSION OF SHARES

    (a) On the death of a member, the survivor or survivors where the member
        was a joint holder, and the legal representatives where he was a sole
        holder, shall be the only persons recognized by the Company as having
        any title to his interest in the shares.

    (b) Any person becoming entitled to a share in consequence of the death
        or insolvency of a member may, upon producing such evidence as may be
        required by the Board, elect either to be registered himself as holder
        of the share or to have some other person nominated by him registered.

================================================================================
                    PART V - FORFEITURE OF SHARES
================================================================================

14. NOTICE ON FAILURE TO PAY CALLS

    If a member fails to pay any call or installment on or before the day
    appointed for payment, the Board may serve a notice requiring payment of
    the amount unpaid together with any interest which may have accrued.

15. FORFEITURE

    If the requirements of any such notice are not complied with, any share
    in respect of which the notice has been given may be forfeited by a
    resolution of the Board.

================================================================================
                    PART VI - ALTERATION OF CAPITAL
================================================================================

16. ALTERATION OF CAPITAL

    The Company may by ordinary resolution:
    (a) Increase the share capital
    (b) Consolidate and divide all or any of its share capital
    (c) Convert all or any of its fully paid-up shares into stock
    (d) Re-convert that stock into fully paid-up shares
    (e) Sub-divide its existing shares into shares of smaller amount
    (f) Cancel shares which have not been taken up

================================================================================
                     PART VII - GENERAL MEETINGS
================================================================================

17. ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING

    The Company shall in each year hold an Annual General Meeting within six
    months from the close of the financial year or within such extended time
    as the Registrar may allow.

18. EXTRAORDINARY GENERAL MEETING

    The Board may convene an Extraordinary General Meeting whenever it thinks
    fit and shall also do so on the requisition of such number of members as
    may be prescribed.

19. NOTICE OF GENERAL MEETING

    (a) A general meeting may be called by giving not less than 21 (twenty-one)
        clear days' notice.

    (b) A general meeting may be called after giving shorter notice if consent
        is given in writing by not less than 95% (ninety-five percent) of the
        members entitled to vote thereat.

================================================================================
              PART VIII - PROCEEDINGS AT GENERAL MEETINGS
================================================================================

20. QUORUM FOR GENERAL MEETING

    The quorum for a general meeting shall be the presence of members holding
    not less than one-third of the total voting power, present in person or
    by proxy, provided that the quorum shall not be less than 2 (two) members
    personally present.

21. CHAIRMAN OF GENERAL MEETING

    The Chairman of the Board shall be the Chairman of every general meeting.
    If there is no such Chairman, or if he is not present within 15 (fifteen)
    minutes after the time appointed for holding the meeting, the members
    present shall choose one of their number to be the Chairman.

22. ADJOURNMENT

    The Chairman may, with the consent of the meeting, adjourn any meeting
    from time to time and from place to place.

================================================================================
                     PART IX - VOTES OF MEMBERS
================================================================================

23. VOTING RIGHTS

    Subject to any rights or restrictions attached to any class of shares,
    on a show of hands every member present in person shall have one vote.
    On a poll, the voting rights of members shall be in proportion to their
    share in the paid-up equity share capital of the Company.

24. PROXY

    Any member entitled to attend and vote at a meeting may appoint another
    person as his proxy to attend and vote instead of him. A proxy need not
    be a member of the Company.

================================================================================
                     PART X - BOARD OF DIRECTORS
================================================================================

25. NUMBER OF DIRECTORS

    The Company shall have a minimum of 2 (two) Directors and a maximum of
    15 (fifteen) Directors.

26. FIRST DIRECTORS

    The following shall be the First Directors of the Company:
    (a) Mr. Rajesh Kumar Mehta (DIN: 07856432)
    (b) Mrs. Priya Sharma (DIN: 08123456)
    (c) Mr. Amit Deshmukh (DIN: 08234567)

27. APPOINTMENT OF DIRECTORS

    (a) Directors shall be appointed by the Company in general meeting.

    (b) The Board may appoint any person as a Director to fill a casual
        vacancy. Such Director shall hold office up to the date of the next
        Annual General Meeting.

28. REMOVAL OF DIRECTORS

    The Company may by ordinary resolution remove any Director before the
    expiry of his period of office, subject to Section 169 of the Act.

29. RESIGNATION OF DIRECTORS

    A Director may resign from his office by giving notice in writing to the
    Company and the Board shall take note of the same.

30. MANAGING DIRECTOR

    The Board may appoint one or more Managing Directors or Whole-time
    Directors and may vest in them powers of management as may be specified.

================================================================================
                PART XI - PROCEEDINGS OF BOARD MEETINGS
================================================================================

31. BOARD MEETINGS

    (a) The Board shall meet at least 4 (four) times in a calendar year with
        a maximum gap of 120 (one hundred and twenty) days between two
        consecutive meetings.

    (b) A meeting of the Board shall be called by giving not less than 7
        (seven) days' notice in writing to every Director.

32. QUORUM FOR BOARD MEETING

    The quorum for a meeting of the Board shall be one-third of its total
    strength or 2 (two) Directors, whichever is higher.

33. CHAIRMAN OF BOARD

    The Board may elect a Chairman from among its members and determine the
    period for which he shall hold office.

34. CASTING VOTE

    In case of an equality of votes, the Chairman shall have a second or
    casting vote.

35. RESOLUTION BY CIRCULATION

    A resolution in writing signed by all Directors shall be as valid and
    effectual as if it had been passed at a meeting of the Board duly convened.

================================================================================
                      PART XII - POWERS OF BOARD
================================================================================

36. GENERAL POWERS

    Subject to the provisions of the Act and these Articles, the business of
    the Company shall be managed by the Board which may exercise all such
    powers of the Company as are not required to be exercised by the Company
    in general meeting.

37. SPECIFIC POWERS

    Without prejudice to the general powers, the Board shall have power:
    (a) To acquire lands, buildings, and properties for development
    (b) To enter into construction contracts and joint development agreements
    (c) To appoint architects, engineers, and consultants
    (d) To obtain project approvals and permits
    (e) To borrow money and give security
    (f) To invest funds of the Company
    (g) To make calls on members
    (h) To appoint and remove officers and employees

================================================================================
                        PART XIII - ACCOUNTS
================================================================================

38. BOOKS OF ACCOUNT

    The Company shall keep proper books of account as required by Section 128
    of the Act.

39. FINANCIAL YEAR

    The financial year of the Company shall be from 1st April to 31st March.

40. FINANCIAL STATEMENTS

    The Board shall lay before the Company at every Annual General Meeting
    the financial statements of the Company.

================================================================================
                         PART XIV - AUDIT
================================================================================

41. APPOINTMENT OF AUDITORS

    Auditors shall be appointed in accordance with Section 139 of the Act.

42. POWERS AND DUTIES OF AUDITORS

    The auditors shall have all powers and duties as provided in Sections 143
    and 144 of the Act.

================================================================================
                   PART XV - DIVIDENDS AND RESERVE
================================================================================

43. DECLARATION OF DIVIDEND

    The Company in general meeting may declare dividends, but no dividend
    shall exceed the amount recommended by the Board.

44. INTERIM DIVIDEND

    The Board may from time to time pay to the members such interim dividends
    as appear to it to be justified by the profits of the Company.

45. DIVIDEND TO BE PAID OUT OF PROFITS

    No dividend shall be paid otherwise than out of profits of the Company.

================================================================================
                       PART XVI - WINDING UP
================================================================================

46. DISTRIBUTION OF ASSETS

    If the Company is wound up, the liquidator may, with the sanction of a
    special resolution of the Company, divide amongst the members the whole
    or any part of the assets of the Company.

================================================================================
                        PART XVII - INDEMNITY
================================================================================

47. INDEMNITY

    Every Director, Manager, Secretary, and other officer of the Company
    shall be indemnified out of the assets of the Company against any
    liability incurred in defending any proceedings, whether civil or criminal,
    in which judgment is given in his favor.
